2.) Any straight line segment connecting two points on a circle or ellipse is called a chord.

3.) A major arc is an arc that is greater than a semicircle. Adjacent angles are two angles that have the same vertex and a common side between them.

4.) A central angle is an angle whose vertex is located at the center of the circle and whose sides are radii. An arc is an unbroken part of the circle.

5.) Intercepted angleThe arc that is inside an inscribed angle and whose endpoints are on the angle. radiusThe distance from the center to the outer rim of a circle.

7.) semi-circle

An arc whose length is exactly one-half of the circumference of the circle is simply called a semi-circle. The line connecting the endpoints of a major arc or minor arc is called a chord.

8.) Proof: The intercepted arc for an angle inscribed in a semi-circle is 180 degrees. Therefore the measure of the angle must be half of 180, or 90 degrees. In other words, the angle is a right angle.

9.) Inscribed angle: An angle whose vertex is a point on a circle and whose sides contain chords. The measure of an inscribed angle is half of the measure of its intercepted arc.

10.) Congruent arcs are arcs on circles with congruent radii that have the same degree measure. A minor arc is an arc whose degree measure is between 0º and 180º. A semicircle is an arc whose degree measure is exactly 180º. A major arc is an arc whose degree measure is between 180º and 360º.

[tex]n_N\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:nitrogen) =\:?[/tex]

[tex]m_N\:(mass\:of\:the\:nitrogen) = 20\:g[/tex]

[tex]MM_N\:(nitrogen\:molar\:mass) = 14\:g/mol[/tex]

[tex]n_N = \dfrac{m_N}{MM_N}[/tex]

[tex]n_N = \dfrac{20\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{14\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{n_N \approx 1.429\:mol}[/tex]

[ Second step ] Let's find the number of mols of Calcium, let's see:

[tex]n_Ca\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:Calcium) =\:?[/tex]

[tex]n_N\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:nitrogen) =1.429\:mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{Ca} = \dfrac{n_N}{2}[/tex]

[tex]n_{Ca} = \dfrac{1.429}{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{n_{Ca} = 0.7145\:mol}[/tex]

[Step Three] Let's find the mass of Calcium in grams, let's see:

[tex]m_{Ca}\:(mass\:of\:the\:Calcium) =\:?\:(en:g)[/tex]

[tex]MM_{Ca}\:(Calcium\:molar\:mass) = 40\:g/mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{Ca} = \dfrac{m_{Ca}}{MM_{Ca}}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]m_{Ca} = n_{Ca}*MM_{Ca}[/tex]

[tex]m_{Ca} = 0.7145\:mol\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\dfrac{\hspace{0.6cm}}{~}*40\:g/mol\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\dfrac{\hspace{0.6cm}}{~}[/tex]

30. B. In ACAN, find the range of possible measures for CN1. CA = 3 and AN = 72. CA = 6 and AN = 11​


Answer:

1. 4 < CN < 7

2. 5 < CN < 17

Step-by-step explanation:

1. ΔCAN: CA = 3, AN = 7, CN = x

Solve using triangle inequality theorem,

CA + AN > CN

3 + 7 > x

10 > x

x < 10

CN + CA > AN

x + 3 > 7

x > 7 - 3

x > 4

CN + AN > CA

x + 7 > 3

x > 7 - 3

x > -4

Taking the intersection of the inequalities, x must be greater than 4 and less than 7 or 4 < x < 7. Since CN = x, 4 < CN < 7

2. ΔCAN: CA = 6, AN = 11, CN = x

Solve using triangle inequality theorem,

CA + AN > CN

6 + 11 > x

17 > x

x < 17

CN + CA > AN

x + 6 > 11

x > 11 - 6

x > 5

CN + AN > CA

x + 11 > 5

x > 6 - 11

x > -5

Taking the intersection of the inequalities, x must be greater than 5 and less than 17 or 5 < x < 17. Since CN = x, 17 < CN < 5
