5. What is abdominal muscular endurance​


Answer:

There are numerous fitness tests for measuring the function of the abdominal muscles (or 'abs' as they are generally called in the fitness industry). This is a very important area for testing, as abdominal strength or abdominal muscle endurance is an indicator of core strength and therefore core stability and support of the lower back.

The tests fall into two main categories, abdominal strength and abdominal endurance (see the list below). In the abdominal strength tests, assessed is the ability of the abdominal muscle to function optimally and hold a correct position under increasing difficulty. In the abdominal endurance tests, assessed is the ability to perform repeated abdominal curls in a set time (e.g. one minute) or at a set rate. Which test you use will be dependent of what you are trying to measure, weighed up against the costs and ease of conducting the test.

21. 2. Abdominal Strength​


Answer:

Abdominal strength refers to the ability of the muscles in the abdominal region to exert force and resist strain. This strength is important for maintaining good posture, supporting the spine, and providing stability for the trunk and torso during various physical activities. Strong abdominal muscles can also improve athletic performance and help prevent injuries. To develop and maintain abdominal strength, exercises such as crunches, planks, and sit-ups can be performed regularly as part of a balanced fitness routine.

28. layers before the abdominal cavity​


Answer:

There is a common set of layers covering and forming all the walls: the deepest being the visceral peritoneum, which covers many of the abdominal organs (most of the large and small intestines, for example), and the parietal peritoneum - which covers the visceral peritoneum below it, the extraperitoneal fat, the transversalis fascia, the internal and external oblique and transversus abdominis aponeurosis, and a layer of fascia, which has different names according to what it covers (e.g., transversalis, psoas fascia).
