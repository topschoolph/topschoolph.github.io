Answer:

55% for food;16,500 of family's monthly income is allotted for food.

Explanation:

As stated in the problem, the family's monthly income is 30,000. There is a given percentage which is allotted to different expenses of the family.

10% is allotted for house rental, which means 3,000 is the allotted budget for house rental.  [30,000x10%=3,000]5% is allotted for health care. Meaning 1,500 is the allotted budget for healthcare. [30,000x5%=1,500]20% is allotted for education. Thus, 6,000 is the allotted budget for education. [30,000x20%=6,000]7% is allotted for daily transportation. Hence, 2,100 is the allotted budget for transportation. [30,000x7%=2,100]3% for savings. Meaning 900 is allotted for savings. [30,000x3%=900]

The total of this allocated budget is 13,500 and the total percentage allotted for the mentioned expenses of the family is 45%. Hence, 55% is the allotted budget for food or 16,500. 30,000 multiplied to 55% is equal to 16,500.

STRONG PARENTAL GUIDANCE

D. Strong family ties and values system

The "tight familial bonds" that result from extended family structures are one of the values that Filipinos are known for. It's because Filipinos think that families should be able to work together and support one another.

Families and friends do everything they can to help each other out, even if it's monetarily. When a relative marries, for example, family members feel obligated to assist out in some way, whether it's with the wedding fees or the labor that goes along with it. Because attending such an event feels like an obligation, the soon-to-be-wed barely needs to issue out invites.

Engaging in family rituals and routines can assist to create a positive environment for mental health. Having regular meals together or enjoying family vacations might be as simple as that.

To develop trust and share burdens, it's critical to spend time with each family member one-on-one.

Stress can be relieved by emotional support from family members.

All Filipino families are known for strong family ties.
