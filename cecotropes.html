What type of response have plants in the desert developed that explains the root system’s large surface area? SCIENCE: EARTH AND LIFE SCIENCES Question and Answer

A cactus in the dry desert is dug up by a scientist and she realizes that the root system covers a large surface area. What type of response have plants in the desert developed that explains the root system’s large surface area?

C) Hydrotropism so the roots can find more water by covering a larger surface

Explanation and Justification C. Hydrotropism

In the desert, where water is scarce, plants have to adapt to survive by developing root systems that can cover a larger surface area to search for water. Hydrotropism is the growth or movement of a plant root in response to water, and it helps the roots to move towards sources of water and to cover a larger surface area to absorb more water.
