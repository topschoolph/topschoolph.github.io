10. Water flowing through a garden hose of radius 3 cm at a speed of 10 cm/s and a sprinkle with 8 holes with 0.75 cm radius per holes is attached to this hose. What is the speed of water per hole in sprinkler?​


Answer:

The speed of water per hole in the sprinkler is 160 cm/s

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the speed of water out of each hole of the sprinkler. In this problem, we are to use the concept of fluid flow in a tube using continuity equation:

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂

where

A₁ and A₂     are the cross section areas at the end of the tube

v₁ and v₂     are the speed of fluid running through the tube

For the given information

r₁ = 3 cm

v₁ = 10 cm/s

r₂ = 0.75 cm

v₂ = ?

Solving the problem

Let us use the equation above to solve for the speed of water in the sprinkler.

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂

Then substitute the given information, we have:

We know that the cross section at the end of a tube and the hole of  a sprinkler is circle, so the area of a circle is A = πr². Let us apply, we have:

(π r₁²)v₁ = (π r₂²)v₂

π (3 cm)² (10 cm/s) = π (0.75 cm)² v₂

π will be cancelled, the solve for v₂ by dividing both sides of the equation by (0.75 cm)² , we have:

[tex]v_{2}=\frac{(3cm)^2(10cm/s)}{(0.75cm)^2}[/tex]

v₂ = 160 cm/s

Therefore, the speed of the water out of each hole in sprinkler is 160 cm/s.

11. Allan observe that water spurt from a can with a hole.Does the height of a hole punched on the side of a can effect how far from the container water would spurt when the can is full of water?


Answer:

Yes

Explanation:

Pressure increases with depth.

Say we have two cans full of water. Can A has Hole A placed higher than how Hole B is placed on Can B.

Water coming from Hole B has more water above it so it has greater pressure than that of Hole A, making the water coming from Hole B travel farther than that of Hole A.
