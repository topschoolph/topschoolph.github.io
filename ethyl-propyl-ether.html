20. Propose a mechanism for the reaction propyle isocyanate with butanol.​


Answer:

Explanation:

One possible mechanism for the reaction of propyle

isocyanate with butanol is:

1. Proton transfer: The reaction starts with the

proton transfer from butanol to the isocyanate

nitrogen, forming a protonated isocyanate

intermediate and a butoxide ion.

Answer:

The reaction between propyl isocyanate and butanol can proceed through an SN2 mechanism, with the isocyanate carbon reacting with the nucleophilic hydroxyl group of butanol. Here is a proposed mechanism:

1. The oxygen of the hydroxyl group of butanol acts as a nucleophile and attacks the carbon atom of the isocyanate group, forming a tetrahedral intermediate.

2. The nitrogen atom of the isocyanate group leaves as a good leaving group, breaking the C-N bond and forming a carbocation intermediate.

3. The carbocation intermediate then undergoes a nucleophilic attack from the oxygen of the hydroxyl group of butanol, forming a new C-O bond and regenerating the hydroxyl group, as well as the formation of the urethane functional group.

4. The reaction is complete, and the product is propyl butyl carbamate (also known as propyl butyl urethane).

Overall reaction:

Propyl Isocyanate + Butanol -> Propyl Butyl Carbamate + Hydrogen Chloride (HCl)

Note that hydrogen chloride may be produced as a byproduct in this reaction.


21. The density of ether is 0.714 g/mL. What is the mass of 34.0 milliliters of ether?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The mass of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{m = dV}[/tex]

where:

d = density

m = mass

V = volume

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

m = dV

m = (0.714 g/mL)(34.0 mL)

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{24.3 g}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of 34.0 mL of ether is 24.3 g.
