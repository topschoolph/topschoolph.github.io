11. Explain the reason behind Rizal’s request to be shot in the small of the back instead of the head?


To display his patriotism and love for his nation, he asked to be shot in the back near the heart. Historians and academics have praised Rizal for his contribution to the independence of the Philippines.

When the Spanish ordered Rizal's execution, he made a final request; according to the received order, the shooter was instructed to shoot him in the head. However, Rizal considered that people who are shot in the head are traitors, hence he was opposed to the concept. He wants to be shot in the back near his heart to demonstrate his patriotism and love for his country. In conclusion, academics say Rizal's killing was unjust, but the Spanish had to carry it out to demonstrate their superiority.

I believe that Rizal's death for the country was well worth it because he sought to aid the Philippines without shedding blood. He desired to assist through his writing. He hoped that by writing about the Spaniards' cruelty, he could inspire the Filipinos to fight back.

13. Which lobe of the brain is located at the back, behind the parietal lobe?​


Answer:

The occipital lobe is behind and slightly underneath the parietal lobe. The parieto-occipital sulcus divides these two lobes. Like the brain itself, the parietal lobe is divided into two hemispheres by the central furrow, or medial longitudinal fissure.
