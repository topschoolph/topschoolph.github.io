23. Determine the force required to accelerate a 2160 kg Ford Expedition from 0 m/s to 27 m/s in 10 seconds? (show your solution)


Answer:

5832 N

Explanation - Solution:

To determine the force required to accelerate a 2160 kg Ford Expedition from 0 m/s to 27 m/s in 10 seconds, we can use the formula:

force = mass x acceleration

First, we need to calculate the acceleration by using the formula:

a = (vf - vi) / t

where:

a = acceleration

vf = final velocity (27 m/s)

vi = initial velocity (0 m/s)

t = time (10 seconds)

a = (27 - 0) / 10

a = 2.7 m/s²

Now we can use this value of acceleration to calculate the force required:

force = mass x acceleration

force = 2160 x 2.7

force = 5832 N

So the force required to accelerate a 2160 kg Ford Expedition from 0 m/s to 27 m/s in 10 seconds is 5832 N.

[tex] \Large \mathbb{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

Let [tex] x[/tex] be the amount (in kilograms) of inert material used. Given that the resulting mixture is [tex]108[/tex] kg, the amount of the fertilizer mixed will be [tex] (108 - x)[/tex] kg.

[tex] \bold{Equation:} [/tex]

Solving for [tex] x[/tex],

[tex] \begin{array}{c} 0.18(108 - x) + 0(x) = 0.10(108) \\ \\ 19.44 - 0.18x = 10.8 \\ \\ 19.44 - 10.8 = 0.18x \\ \\ 8.64 = 0.18x \\ \\ x = \dfrac{8.64}{0.18} \\ \\ \boxed{x = 48} \end{array} [/tex]

Thus, [tex] 48 [/tex] kg of inert material is used.
