6. It is a cable that combines the techniques of shielding, cancellation, and wire twisting.​


Answer:

Shielded twisted pair (STP) cable combines the technique of shielding, cancellation and wire twisting. Each pair of wires is wrapped in a metallic foil. The four pairs of wires then are wrapped in an overall metallic braid of foil. ... This kind of cable is often applied between equipment, racks and buildings.


7. "What copper-cored cable is surrounded by a heavy shielding? a. coaxial cable c. optical fiber b. fiber optics d. twisted pair"


The copper-cored cable surrounded by a heavy shielding is Coaxial Cable.  

What is a Coaxial Cable?

A Coaxial cable or coax is a type of electrical cable that has an inner conductor surrounded by a tubular insulating layer and a tubular conducting shield. It also conducts electrical signals using an inner conductor surrounded by an insulating layer and all enclosed by a shield, most likely one to four layers of woven metallic braid and metallic tape.  

What it is used for?

Coaxial cable is a versatile transmission medium, used in a wide variety of applications. It is used to transmit lines for radio frequency, long-distance telephone transmission, local area network, and cable television distribution.

20. 7. Which of the following types of twisted-pair cables rely solely on the cancellation effect? A. Shielded Twisted Pair B. Unshielded Twisted Pair C. Twisted Pair D. Fiber Optic ​


Answer:

B. UNSHIELDED TWISTED PAIR

Explanation:

Unshielded twisted-pair (UTP) - Cable that has two or four pairs of wires. This type of cable relies solely on the cancellation effect produced by the twisted-wire pairs that limits signal degradation caused by electromagnetic interference (EMI) and radio frequency interference (RFI).


21. What is the reason behind twists of each pair in a twisted pair cable?​


Answer:

Twisted pairs are made up of two insulated copper wires that are twisted together. The twisting is done to help cancel exterior electromagnetic interference. Crosstalk interference can come from other pairs within a cable. Each type of twisted pair cable has bandwidth and data rate specifications.
