Boyle's LawP1V1 = P2V2

where in, P1 = first pressure

V1 = first volume

P2 = second pressure

V2 = second volume

1)

Given: P1 = 1.50atm

V1 = 1.25L

P2 = 2.50atm

Unknown: V2 = ?

Formula: V2 = P1V1 / P2

Note: To get the formula, apply division and cancellation of like terms using the original formula of Boyle's Law.

• P1V1 = P2V2 ← divide both sides by P2

• P1V1/P2 = P2V2/P2 ← cancel like terms

• P1V1/P2 = V2 ← final formula

Solution:

V2 = P1V1 / P2

= (1.50atm)(1.25L) / 2.50atm

= 1.875atm•L / 2.50atm

= 0.75L

Note: The unit for pressure is cancelled out, so the unit for volume will remain which is Liters(L).

Final Answer: V2 = 0.75L

2)

Given: V1 = 1.22L

P1 = 0.96atm

V2 = 2.63L

Unknown: P2 = ?

Formula: P2 = P1V1 / V2

Note: Apply the same method I used above to get the formula for what is asked.

• P1V1 = P2V2 ← divide both sides by V2

• P1V1/V2 = P2V2/V2 ← cancel like terms

• P1V1/V2 = P2 ← final formula

Solution:

P2 = P1V1 / V2

= (0.96atm)(1.22L) / 2.63L

= 1.1712atm•L / 2.63L

= 0.45atm

Note: This time, the unit for volume is cancelled out, so the unit for pressure will remain which is atmosphere(atm).

Final Answer: P2 = 0.45atm

Tip: Cancellation of like terms is applicable when the other like term is on the denominator.

SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{0.918 atm} \\ & V = \text{2.22 L} \\ & T = 45^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{318 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

23. Problem 2: The volume of a helium balloon is 0.85 L at 1.0 atm. How much is the change in the volume of the helium balloon if the pressure is increased to 2.0 atm? He V₁=0.85 L P₁= 1.0 atm He V₂= ? P₂= 2.0 atm​


This problem can be solved using Boyle's Law, which states that at a constant temperature, the volume of a gas is inversely proportional to its pressure.

Boyle's Law can be expressed mathematically as P₁V₁ = P₂V₂, where P₁ and V₁ are the initial pressure and volume, and P₂ and V₂ are the final pressure and volume.

Given:

He V₁ = 0.85 L

P₁ = 1.0 atm

P₂ = 2.0 atm

To find: He V₂ (change in volume of the helium balloon)

Using Boyle's Law:

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

(1.0 atm)(0.85 L) = (2.0 atm)(He V₂)

0.85 L = 2.0 He V₂

He V₂ = 0.85 L / 2.0

He V₂ = 0.425 L

Therefore, the change in volume of the helium balloon is 0.425 L.

26. What volume will 2.50 L of a gas occupy if the pressure is changed from 0.9 atm to 0.6 atm?​


To solve this problem, we can use the combined gas law, which relates the pressure, volume, and temperature of a gas:

(P1 x V1)/T1 = (P2 x V2)/T2

where P1, V1, and T1 are the initial pressure, volume, and temperature of the gas, and P2, V2, and T2 are the final pressure, volume, and temperature of the gas.

We can rearrange this equation to solve for V2:

V2 = (P1 x V1 x T2)/(P2 x T1)

Substituting the given values, we get:

V2 = (0.9 atm x 2.50 L x T2)/(0.6 atm x T1)

Since the temperature is not given and assuming that it remains constant, we can cancel out the T2 and T1 terms:

V2 = (0.9 atm x 2.50 L)/(0.6 atm)

V2 = 3.75 L

Therefore, if the pressure is changed from 0.9 atm to 0.6 atm, 2.50 L of the gas will occupy a volume of 3.75 L.

28. What should be the final temperature of a 25.0 L sample of hydrogen gas at 1.00 atm and 35°C if it is compressed to a volume of 20.0 L and a pressure of 2.00 atm?


This problem is an example of a Combined Gas Law. Let us first discuss the gas Laws that is involved in this problem before we answer the question.

According to Charle’s Law, the temperature of the gas is directly proportional to the Volume of the gas.

V=T or V1/T1 = V2/T2

According to the Boyle’s Law, the pressure of the gas is inversely proportional to the volume of the gas.

P=1/V or P1V1 = P2V2

According to Gay-Lussac Law, the pressure of the gas is directly proportional to the temperature of the gas.

P=T or P1/T1 = P2/T2

Having these gas laws, Let us summarize and combined them.

PV=T or P1V1/T1 = P2V2 /T2
