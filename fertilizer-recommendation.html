16. determine the amount of fertilizer to useto meet the recommended rate for phosphorus 0-80-0 determine the fertilizer to use then commpute the weight of fertilizer material and convert into number of bags​


Answer:

To determine the amount of fertilizer to use to meet the recommended rate for phosphorus 0-80-0, we need to know the recommended rate (in pounds per acre) and the size of the area to be fertilized.

Let's say the recommended rate is 20 pounds per acre and the area to be fertilized is 2 acres.

To calculate the amount of fertilizer needed, we would use the following formula:

(Recommended rate (in pounds per acre) x Area to be fertilized (in acres)) / Percentage of phosphorus in fertilizer

So, in this case it would be:

(20 x 2) / 0.8 = 50 pounds of fertilizer

To convert this into number of bags, we need to know the weight of each bag of fertilizer. Let's say each bag is 50 pounds.

50 pounds of fertilizer / 50 pounds per bag = 1 bag of fertilizer

So, we would need to use 1 bag of fertilizer to meet the recommended rate for phosphorus 0-80-0 for 2 acres.

Answer:

Soil Fertility Guide

Calculating Fertilizer Rates from Nutrient Recommendations

Soil test recommendations are given in lb/ac or kg/ha of nutrients. To determine the fertilizer rate for a particular nutrient, multiply the rate of the desired nutrient by 100 and divide by the percentage of the nutrient in the fertilizer.

Example 1

Recommended rate of N is 80 lb/ac Using 46-0-0, the rate of fertilizer required is: (80 x 100) / 46 = 174 lb/ac

Example 2

Recommended rate of P 2O 5 is 40 lb/ac Using 11-52-0, the rate of fertilizer required is: (40 x 100) / 52 = 77 lb/ac 77 lb/ac of 11-52-0 would also supply (11/100) x 77 = 8.5 lb/ac of N.

Example 3

Recommended rate of K 2O is 15 lb/ac. Using 0-0-60, the rate of fertilizer required is: (15 x 100) / 60 = 25 lb/ac
