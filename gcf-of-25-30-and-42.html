5. Show your solution by using continuous division method to find the gcf of 25, 30 and 42


GREATEST COMMON FACTOR (GCF) WITH CONTINUOUS DIVISION METHOD

The GCF by continuous division method of 25, 30, and 42 is 1.

What is the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)?

The Greatest Common Factor (also known as GCF) is the largest expression that is a factor of all the expressions.

In other words, the largest positive integer that divides all the given numbers evenly and without leaving any remainder is known as the greatest common factor (GCF) of a set of whole numbers.

The GCF of two positive integers m and n is the greatest positive integers x, a factor of both m and n. Denote with:

GCF(m, n) = x

How to Find GCF?

These are the three methods for determining the greatest common factor between two numbers, and more non-zero integers:

Listing out common factorsPrime factorizationContinuous division method

GCF by Continuous Division Method

Finding the GCF with lists or a factor can be slow and laborious when the numbers are large. To determine the highest common factors, the continuous division would be a quicker way.

Here are the steps with example 1:

Find the GCF of 24 and 30.

2 |24   30 |                1. Divide the number by common factors

3 |12     15 |

   |4       5  |                2. Stop when there are no common factors

3. The GCF  is produced by multiplying the numbers in the left column together

2 · 3 = 6

Therefore, the GCF of 24 and 30 is 6.

GCF(24, 30) = 6.

Example 2:

Find the GCF of 24, 36, and 30.

2 |24   36   48 |               1. Divide the number by common factors

2 |12    18    24 |            

3 |6     9     12   |

  |2     3      4    |               2. Stop when there are no common factors

3. The GCF  is produced by multiplying the numbers in the left column together

2 · 2  · 3 = 12

Therefore, the GCF of 24, 36, and 48 is 12.

GCF(24, 36, 48) = 1.
