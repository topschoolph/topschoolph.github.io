4. Hemoglobin is the protein that transports O2 through the blood from the lungs to the rest of the body. In doing so, each molecule of hemoglobin combines with four moelcules of O2. Of 1.00-g of hemoglobin combines with 1.53 mL of O2 at 37 C and 743 torr, what is the molar mass of hemoglobin? write your answer into three decimal places with g/mol as its unit like this format 34,234 g/mol​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{743 torr = 0.97763158 atm} \\ & V = \text{1.53 mL = 0.00153 L} \\ & T = 37^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{310.15 K} \\ & mass_{\text{Hb}} = \text{1.00 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} PV & = nRT \\ nRT & = PV \\ \frac{nRT}{RT} & = \frac{PV}{RT} \\ n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.97763157895 atm})(\text{0.00153 L})}{\left(0.082057366081 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{310.15 K})} \\ & = \text{0.0000587729235125 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of hemoglobin.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{Hb}} & = \text{0.0000587729235125 mol} \: \text{O}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol Hb}}{\text{4 mol} \: \text{O}_2} \\ & = \text{0.000014693230878125 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the molar mass of hemoglobin.

[tex]\begin{aligned} MM_{\text{Hb}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{Hb}}}{n_{\text{Hb}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{1.00 g}}{\text{0.000014693230878125 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{68,058.551 g/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar mass of hemoglobin is 68,058.551 g/mol.

Of 1.00-g of hemoglobin combines with 1.53 mL of O2 at 37 C and 743 torr, what is the molar mass of hemoglobin? write your answer into three decimal places with g/mol as its unit like this format 34,234 g/mol​


Answer:

n = PV/RT

P = pressure = 724 torr x 1 atm/760 torr = 0.953 atm

V = volume in L = 2.13 ml x 1 L/1000 ml = 0.00213 L

R = gas constant = 0.0821 Latm/Kmol

T = temp in K = 37º + 273 = 310K

n = (0.953)(0.00213) / (0.0821)(310)

n = 7.98x10-5 moles of O2 molecules

moles Hgb molecules = 7.98x10-5 moles O2 x 1 mol Hgb/4 mol O2 = 1.99x10-5 moles Hgb molecules

molar mass Hgb = g / moles

molar mass Hgb = 1.37 g /1.99x10-5 moles = 68,844 g/mol  (be sure to check the math)
