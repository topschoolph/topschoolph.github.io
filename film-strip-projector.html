3. 5 facts about film projectors.


Answer:

The 5 fact's in film projectors is:

1. A movie projector is a machine that shows moving pictures by projecting them on a projection screen.

2. The first movie projector was the Zoopraxiscope, invented by British photographer Eadweard Muybridge in 1879.

3. The zoopraxiscope projected images from rotating glass disks in rapid succession to give the impression of motion.

4. A more sophisticated movie projector was invented by Frenchman Louis Le Prince while working in Leeds.

20. explain how celluloid strip film contributed to the evolution of film making


How Celluloid Strip Film Contributed to the Evolution of Film Making

Celluloid Strip Film contributed to the evolution of film making by being the right medium for recording pictures due to its durability. Since the discovery of its property, it paved way to the development of motion-picture camera. The first visual recorder device embedded with celluloid strip film was invented by Louis Le Prince. However, due to his disappearance, the creation of motion-picture camera with celluloid strip film was not credited to him but to William Kennedy Laurie Dickson.

Timeline of the Beginning of Film Making Industry

With the invention of celluloid film strip in the late 1880s, film making essentially found its way to the world of arts and entertainment.

Dickson, under the supervision of Thomas Edison, invented Kinetoscope implanted with celluloid strip film in 1888.Lumiere brothers invented a projector (bigger than a kinetograph) called as cinematograph that serves as a camera, printer, and projector in 1895.Between 1896 and 1904, production companies began to rise and directors are being hired.At the start of 1900s, editing of films was initiated.In 1905, the first movie theater opened in Pittsuburg.

23. what is the similarities of overhead projectors and slide projectors?​


Answer:

Overhead projectors usually work with transparency slides. These are usually letter-sized sheets of transparent film that can be printed or marked on. Transparencies can be printed in full color and allow both permanent and erasable markers, but they’re relatively fixed. After all, you can’t un-print them in the middle of a presentation. Since data projectors present what gets sent to them, they have no limits on what you can project or how much you can change it. If your computer or other video sources can create it, a data projector can show it.

30. how the celluloid strip film technological advances contributed to the evloution of filmmaking


Answer

The contributions of a Celluloid Film Strip:

It enabled a camera to capture photographs of a moving subject into the film using only a single camera.Celluloid Films were easier to manage compared to paper films because of their transparency. Celluloid films do not crumple like paper and are match easier to roll. This made it easier for early motion capture production.It did not only contribute to movie making but to medicine as well. It was also used as films for X-raysOther Info

Celluloid films were made of cellulose nitrate and camphor which are highly flammable substances. This eventually leads to the replacement of celluloid film strips with thermoplastics which were more resistant to flame and easier to handle. Today, every film are produced digitally but Film developing still exists as a Hobby.
