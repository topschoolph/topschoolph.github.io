2. 2. How do scientists systematically arrange electromagnetic waves?​


Answer:

The scientist systematically organize electromagnetic waves by their frequency and wavelength. This is because the sun, earth, and other bodies emit electromagnetic energy that varies with wavelengths. As a result, the spectrum of the waves is divided into sections based on the wavelength.

Explanation:

The electromagnetic spectrum is the range of frequencies (the spectrum) of electromagnetic radiation and their respective wavelengths and photon energies. Although all electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light in a vacuum, they do so at a wide range of frequencies, wavelengths, and photon energies. The electromagnetic spectrum covers the range of all electromagnetic radiation and consists of many subrange, commonly referred to as components, such as visible light or ultraviolet radiation. Different components carry different names based on differences in the emission, transmission, absorption behavior of the corresponding waves and their different practical applications. There are no precise accepted boundaries between any of the contiguous parts, so the ranges tend to overlap.
