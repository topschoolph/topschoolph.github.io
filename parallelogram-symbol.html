7. Rectangles are parallelograms, but parallelograms are not rectangles.​


Answer:

Since it has two sets of parallel sides and two pairs of opposite sides that are congruent, a rectangle has all of the properties of a parallelogram. That's why a rectangle is always a parallelogram. However, a parallelogram is not always a rectangle.

→ A quadrilateral is a four-sided polygon while a parallelogram is a quadrilateral with two sets of opposite sides that are parallel.

→ A parallelogram is a quadrilateral that:

a. A quadrilateral's diagonals are bisected by each other.

b. The opposite sides of both pairs of opposite sides are congruent.

c. Both opposite angle pairs are congruent.

d. Both pairs of sides are congruent and parallel.

e. Both successive angles are supplementary to a quadrilateral's angle.

30. Properties of Parallelograms1. Opposite sides of parallelogram are congruent.2. Opposite angles of parallelogram are congruent.​


Answer:

1. If a quadrilateral is a parallelogram, then its opposite sides are congruent. If a quadrilateral is a parallelogram, then its opposite angles are congruent. If a quadrilateral is a parallelogram, then its diagonals bisect each other. If a quadrilateral is a parallelogram, then consecutive angles are supplementary.

2. Opposite Angles Are Congruent

The angles opposite of each other will have the same measurement. It's common for a parallelogram to have two acute angles and two obtuse angles. Therefore, the acute angles should have the same measurement, and the obtuse angles should also have the same measurement.
