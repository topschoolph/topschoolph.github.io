26. what is the function of the nerve endings in the skin​


Answer:

Free nerve endings are the most common nerve endings in skin, and they extend into the middle of the epidermis. Free nerve endings are sensitive to painful stimuli, to hot and cold, and to light touch. They are slow to adjust to a stimulus and so are less sensitive to abrupt changes in stimulation.

28. are all feelings are detected by the same nerve endings?


Explanation:

No, not all feelings are detected by the same nerve endings. Different types of sensory receptors in the body are specialized to detect different types of stimuli, such as touch, temperature, pain, and pressure.

For example, the nerve endings that detect pressure are different from those that detect pain. Similarly, the nerve endings that detect temperature are different from those that detect touch.

So while all of these different types of sensory receptors ultimately send signals to the brain to create our perception of feelings, they do so through different pathways and using different nerve endings.
