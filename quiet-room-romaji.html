22. 1. What is the difference in terms of usage among Hiragana, katakana, and romaji?​


Answer:

The major difference between hiragana and katakana is the fact that hiragana is primarily used to represent Japanese words, while katakana represents foreign words. Japanese is a language with many borrowed words, and katakana immediately alerts the reader to the fact that the word is an imported one.

24. 勇気 (Romaji: yuuki) means love. -False


The statement is truly wrong or false.

It is a Japanese kanji meaning courage, bravery, valor, nerve, boldness. Yūki in romanji. The first kanji figure (勇, Isamu) literally means courage, cheer up, be in high spirits, bravery, heroism. While the second kanji figure (気, Ki) means spirit, mind, air, atmosphere, mood.
