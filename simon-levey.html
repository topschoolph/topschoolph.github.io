2. how to explain no error/normal in levey jennings chart??​


Answer:

Levey-Jennings charts are used to track the performance of a given quality control sample over time. On these charts, each data point represents the mean of a given quality control sample, and the error/normal range is plotted as two horizontal lines above and below the mean. These two lines indicate the range of acceptable performance for that quality control sample. For any data point falling within this range, we would conclude that the sample is performing normally and there is no error.

Answer:

A Levey-Jennings chart is a graphical representation of quality control data used in laboratory testing. It is used to monitor the accuracy and precision of a laboratory test over time. The chart plots the test results on the y-axis and the date or time of the test on the x-axis.

When there is no error or abnormality in the laboratory test, the data points on the Levey-Jennings chart will fall within the control limits. The control limits are calculated based on the mean and standard deviation of the test results obtained during the quality control process. The control limits are usually set at 2 standard deviations above and below the mean.

If the data points fall within the control limits, it indicates that the test is performing consistently and accurately over time. This means that the laboratory test is producing reliable and accurate results, and there is no need for corrective action.

However, if the data points fall outside the control limits, it indicates that there is a problem with the test. This could be due to errors in the testing process, equipment malfunction, or other factors. In this case, corrective action is needed to identify and address the problem to ensure that the test results are accurate and reliable.

18. batas underwood-simons


Ang Batas Underwood-Simmons o mas kilala rin bilang Revenue Act of 1913 ng Estados Unidos ay ang pagsasabalik ng pederal na buwis at pagbababa ng taripa mula 40% tungo 25%, na naunang naipataw sa pamamagitan ng Payne-Aldrich Tariff Act ng 1909.

 

Sa ilalim din ng Underwood-Simmons Act, tinanggal na ang mga limitasyon sa mga produktong nilalabas ng Pilipinas papasok ng Estados Unidos gaya ng asukal at tabako.
