1. the midsegment theorem of trapezoid​


-----------------------------------------------------------

Answer:

The Trapezoid Midsegment Theorem states that a line segment connecting the midpoints of the legs of the trapezoid is parallel to the bases, and equal to half their sum.


2. how to get the midsegment of a trapezoid?


The measure of the midsegment of a trapezoid is equal to the average of its two bases.

ms = [tex] \frac{1}{2} [/tex] b₁b₂
where ms is the measure of the midsegment, b₁ and b₂ are the bases

7. what to do to find the midsegment of a trapezoid when given only the measuremen of 1 of the bases and how to find 2 of the bases when only given the midsegment?​


Answer:

To find the midsegment of a trapezoid when given only the measurement of one of the bases, follow these steps:

1. Draw the trapezoid and label the given base with its measurement.

2. Draw a line segment connecting the midpoints of the two non-parallel sides of the trapezoid. This line segment is the midsegment of the trapezoid.

3. Label the length of the midsegment as "m".

4. Use the formula for the midsegment of a trapezoid, which is:

m = (a + b) / 2

where "a" and "b" are the lengths of the two parallel bases.

Rearrange the formula to solve for the unknown base:

b = 2m - a

To find the lengths of the two bases of a trapezoid when only given the midsegment, follow these steps:

1. Draw the trapezoid and label the midsegment with its measurement.

2. Draw a line segment connecting the midpoints of the two non-parallel sides of the trapezoid. This line segment is the midsegment of the trapezoid.

3. Label the lengths of the two parallel bases as "a" and "b".

4. Use the formula for the midsegment of a trapezoid, which is:

m = (a + b) / 2

Rearrange the formula to solve for one of the unknown bases:

a = 2m - b

or

b = 2m - a

Substitute the given value of the midsegment and solve for the unknown base. Repeat the process to find the second base.


8. In trapezoid, the lower base is represented as c^2+2 and the upper base as c^2+6 while the midsegment is 4c. Find the leght of the midsegment.​


• Problem:

In trapezoid, the lower base is represented as c²+2 and the upper base as c²+6 while the midsegment is 4c. Find the length of the midsegment.

• Solution:

To find the length of the midsegment, let's try to use this formula.

[tex] \tt m = \frac{(c {}^{2} + 2 )+ (c {}^{2} + 6) }{2} \\ [/tex]

Let's solve for the value of c.

[tex] \tt 4c= \frac{(c {}^{2} + 2 )+ (c {}^{2} + 6) }{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt 4c= \frac{c {}^{2} + 2 + c {}^{2} + 6 }{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt 4c= \frac{2c {}^{2} + 8 }{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt 4c= c {}^{2} + 4\\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt 0= c {}^{2} - 4c + 4\\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt 0= ( c - 2) {}^{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt c - 2= 0\\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt c = 2\\ [/tex]

Thus, the midsegment is 4c = 4(2) or 8.

• Answer:

The length of the midsegment is 8.
