6. An object rests on a horizontal floor. The coefficient static friction is 0.4 and acceleration of gravity is 9.8 m/s2. Determine (a) The maximum force of the static friction (b) The minimum force of F


Answer

step by step explanation:

Mass (m) = 1 kg

The coefficient static friction (μs) = 0.4

The acceleration of gravity (g) = 9.8 m/s2

Weight (w) = m g = (1 kg)(10 m/s2) = 10 kg m/s2 = 10 Newton

Normal force (N) = w = 10 Newton

Wanted :

(a) The maximum force of the static friction (b) The minimum force of F

Solution :

(a) The maximum force of the static friction

fs = μs N

fs = (0.4)(9.8 N) = 3.92 Newton

(b) The minimum force of F

If the force F is exerted on the object but the object isn’t moved, so there must be the force of static friction exerted by the floor on the object. If the object will start to move, the force of the static friction is exceeded, there must be the force of the kinetic friction. Object start moves if F is greater than the maximum force of the static friction.

So the minimum force of F = maximum force of the static friction = 3.92 Newton.

27. What is the coefficient of static friction if it takes 44 n of force to move a box that weighs 86 n?


The coefficient of static friction is 0.51 n.

Ff = 44N

Fn = 86N

Ff = μFn

44 = μ86

μ = [tex]\frac{44}{86}[/tex]

μ = 0.51

FRICTION

In either the same direction as the motion or in the opposite direction, friction acts as a force. in the opposite direction as the forces that are exerting themselves.

One force that works against the motion is friction. Friction is the force between two surfaces that acts in a direction perpendicular to the motion of the object between them.

In the context of friction, an example is:

Walking - Friction is the reason our feet don't slip when we're walking on a smooth surface. However, when walking on ice or other similarly slippery surfaces, our grip weakens and we lose our footing.

The coefficient of friction, denoted by fr, is the ratio of the resistive force of friction, Fr, to the normal or perpendicular force, N, that acts to bring the two objects together. The corresponding equation is fr = Fr/N. No value of the friction coefficient greater than 1 is possible.
