6. A toll bridge carries 10,000 veh/day. The current toll is $3.00/vehicle. Studies have shown that for each increase in the toll of 50 cents, the traffic volume will decrease by 1000 veh/day. It is desired to increase the toll to a point where revenue will be maximized.I. Write the expression for travel demand on the bridge related to toll increase and current volume.II. Determine toll charges to maximize revenues.III. Determine traffic in veh/day after toll increase.IV. Determine total revenue increase with new toll.


Answer:I. The expression for travel demand on the bridge related to toll increase and current volume can be represented as:

TD = 10,000 - 2,000(x - 3.00)

Where TD is the travel demand in vehicles per day, x is the toll in dollars, and the expression (x-3.00) represents the number of 50 cent increments by which the toll is increased.

The decrease in travel demand for each 50 cent increment is 1,000 vehicles per day, so the total decrease in travel demand for x dollars is (x-3.00) * 2,000.

Therefore, the travel demand TD is the difference between the initial travel demand of 10,000 vehicles per day and the decrease in travel demand due to the toll increase.

II. To determine the toll charges that maximize revenue, we need to find the point where the revenue function reaches its maximum value. The revenue function is given by:

R(x) = x(TD)

Substituting the expression for TD into the revenue function, we get:

R(x) = x(10,000 - 2,000(x - 3.00))

Expanding and simplifying, we get:

R(x) = -2,000x^2 + 20,000x - 30,000

To find the toll charges that maximize revenue, we need to take the derivative of the revenue function with respect to x and set it equal to zero:

R'(x) = -4,000x + 20,000 = 0

Solving for x, we get:

x = $5.00

Therefore, the toll charges that maximize revenue is $5.00.

III. To determine the traffic volume after the toll increase, we need to substitute the toll charges of $5.00 into the expression for TD:

TD = 10,000 - 2,000($5.00 - $3.00) = 8,000 vehicles per day.

Therefore, the traffic volume after the toll increase is 8,000 vehicles per day.

IV. To determine the total revenue increase with the new toll, we need to substitute the toll charges of $5.00 and the traffic volume of 8,000 vehicles per day into the revenue function:

R($5.00) = $5.00(8,000) = $40,000

The total revenue increase with the new toll is $40,000 - $30,000 = $10,000.
