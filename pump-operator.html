11. 1. Describe the construction and operation of in-line fuel injection pumps?​


Answer:

QUESTION:

1. Describe the construction and operation of in-line fuel injection pumps?

ANSWER:

An Injection Pump is the device that pumps fuel into the cylinders of a diesel engine. Traditionally, the injection pump was driven indirectly from the crankshaft by gears, chains or a toothed belt (often the timing belt) that also drives the camshaft. It rotates at half crankshaft speed in a conventional four-stroke diesel engine. Its timing is such that the fuel is injected only very slightly before top dead centre of that cylinder's compression stroke. It is also common for the pump belt on gasoline engines to be driven directly from the camshaft. In some systems injection pressures can be as high as 620 bar (8992 psi) [1] .

28. Make a paragraph on how to operate gasoline water pump. ​


Answer:

1. How to start a gasoline water pump? For 2in /3in /4in Caution: never attempt to operate the pump without priming water, or the pump will overheat. Remove plug. Fill water and Install plug.

2. Check the fuel level by opening the tank and visually inspecting.

3. Check engine oil. Ensure the Pump is on a level surface. Running the Engine with a low oil level can cause engine damage.

4. Turn the engine switch to the ON position.

5. Rotate the fuel valve lever to ON position (to the right). Push the air choke lever to the CLOSE position out to close the choke(to the left). Hold the frame by the left hand. Grasp the recoil starter handle and fast pull it until the engine starts. Return the handle slowly by hand.

6. Immediately push the choke lever to the OPEN position (to the right) as the engine warms up. If the engine still does not start, push the Air choke lever to the middle position and then try start again.
