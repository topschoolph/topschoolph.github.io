3. Assignment # 1 Name: Give the parts of a polygon Decagon ABCDEFGHIJ Vertices Angles Sides Consecutive Vertices Consecutive Angles Consecutive Sides Diagonals ABC and D​


Here are the parts of a decagon (10-sided polygon) ABCDEFGHIJ, along with their definitions:

Vertices: The points where the sides of the polygon meet. In this case, the vertices are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, and J.

Angles: The amount of rotation between adjacent sides of the polygon. In a regular decagon, all angles have the same measure. Each angle in a regular decagon measures 144 degrees.

Sides: The line segments that form the polygon. In this case, the sides are AB, BC, CD, DE, EF, FG, GH, HI, IJ, and JA.

Consecutive Vertices: Two vertices that are adjacent to each other along a side of the polygon. In this case, examples of consecutive vertices include AB, BC, CD, and so on.

Consecutive Angles: Two angles that are adjacent to each other along a side of the polygon. In this case, examples of consecutive angles include the angle at A and the angle at B, the angle at B and the angle at C, and so on.

Consecutive Sides: Two sides that are adjacent to each other along a vertex of the polygon. In this case, examples of consecutive sides include AB and BC, BC and CD, CD and DE, and so on.

Diagonals: A line segment that connects two non-adjacent vertices of the polygon. In this case, examples of diagonals include AD, BE, CF, and so on. Note that ABC and DEF are also diagonals of the decagon.
