23. The second term of an arithmetic sequence is 24 and the fifth term is 3. find the first term and the common difference.


Let’s assume that 24 is our first term. We just subtracted 1 from the value of its n. Therefore, we also have to assume that 3 is our fourth term.

Consider the Arithmetic formula:
an = a1 + (n-1) d [the first n and 1 are subscripts)


We have to find the common difference ‘d’.

Just substitute values to the variables.
an (where n is a subscript) is the last term and should be substituted by 3. a1 is the first term and should be substituted by 24. The variable n should be substituted by 4 since there are 4 terms and our ‘an’ is substituted by the fourth term.

Our equation should looks like this:
3 = 24 + (4 - 1) d

Now, we have to solve for d.

3 - 24 = 3d
-21 = 3d
-7 = d

So the common difference is -7.

Now we have to find the first term. But before that, we have to make 24 as the second term and 3 as the fifth term back. Then, substitute ‘an’ by 24 and ‘n’ by 2. (Or we can use the fifth term and substitute n by 5)

an = a1 + (n-1) d
24 = a1 + (2 - 1) (-7)
24 = a1 + (-7)
24 + 7 = a1
31 = a1

Therefore, the first term is 31 and the common difference is -7.

30. The second term of an arithmetic sequence is 24 and the fifth term is 3. Find the first term and the common difference.​


Answer:

The first term (a₁) is 31.The common difference  (d) is -7.

Step-by-step explanation:

a₂ = 24  ⇒ n=2

a₅ = 3 ⇒ n=5

Arithmetic sequence general rule:

an = a₁ + (n-1)(d)

a₂ = 24  ⇒ n=2:

24 = a₁ + (2-1)(d)

a₁ = -d + 24  (Equation 1)

a₅ = 3 ⇒ n=5

3 = a₁ + (5-1)(d)

a₁ = - 4d + 3  (Equation 2)

Find d:

-4d + 3 = -d + 24

4d - d = -24 + 3

3d/3 = -21/3

d = -7

The common difference (d) is 7.

Find the first term, a₁:

a₁ = -d+24

a₁ = -(-7) + 24

a₁ = 7 + 24

a₁ = 31

The first term is 31.
