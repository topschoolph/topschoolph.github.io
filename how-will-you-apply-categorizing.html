There are several techniques and theories in categorization. Apart from that, from a historical point of view, there are three general approaches to the categorization in question, namely:

Classic categorizationConcept groupingprototype theory

EXPLANATION:

1. Classic categorization

Classical categorization spoke in the context of Western philosophy by the scientist Plato who introduced an approach to classifying objects based on the similarity of features in the dialogues of statesmen. This approach was deepened and developed by Aristotle in the work on categories, in which he further explored the difference between objects and classes.

2. Concept grouping

The grouping of concepts or assumptions is an update to the classical approach resulting from activities to explain how knowledge is described. In this category, classes (entities or groups) are created by initially formulating a conceptual description, then classifying the concept according to that description. This conceptual categorization was created as a machine paradigm for unsupervised learning.

3. Prototype Theory

Categorizing can also be considered as a process of collecting prototype-based objects, namely the notion of necessary conditions. It has also been suggested that this prototype-based categorization is the basis for human development, even learning based on learning about the world in the womb. The cognitive approach is that existing categories are stratified and inconsistent in the ranking of their members.
