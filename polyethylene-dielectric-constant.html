3. How does the polar character of water molecules help to explain water’s relatively large dielectric constant?


Answer:

Water molecules are polar, meaning that they have a positive charge on one end (the hydrogen atoms) and a negative charge on the other end (the oxygen atom). This polarity arises from the unequal distribution of electrons within the water molecule, with the oxygen atom having a greater affinity for electrons than the hydrogen atoms.

The polar nature of water molecules allows them to interact with each other through electrostatic forces, and this interaction is one of the factors that contributes to water's relatively large dielectric constant. The dielectric constant is a measure of a substance's ability to polarize in response to an applied electric field, and water has a relatively large dielectric constant compared to many other substances.

In water, the polar nature of the molecules allows them to align with an applied electric field, which creates a separation of charge within the water molecules. This separation of charge increases the overall dipole moment of the water molecules and leads to a stronger electric field within the water. As a result, water has a higher dielectric constant compared to non-polar substances, which do not have a permanent dipole moment and are less able to polarize in response to an applied electric field.

Overall, the polar character of water molecules plays a significant role in water's relatively large dielectric constant, as it allows the molecules to interact with each other through electrostatic forces and polarize in response to an applied electric field.

26. Two coins lie 1.5m apart in water they carry identical charges approximately how large is the charge on each of the coin if the force is 2N and dielectric constant is 80


Explanation:

The force between two charged objects is equal to the product of their charges divided by the dielectric constant of the medium between them, multiplied by the distance between them. Therefore, we can write the equation F = (q1 * q2) / (k * d) to find the force between two charged objects, where F is the force, q1 and q2 are the charges on the two objects, k is the dielectric constant of the medium between them, and d is the distance between them.

In the given problem, we are told that the two coins are 1.5 meters apart, the force between them is 2 newtons, and the dielectric constant of the medium (water) is 80. We are also told that the coins carry identical charges. Therefore, we can substitute these values into the equation above to find the charge on each coin:

2 = (q1 * q1) / (80 * 1.5)

2 = q1^2 / 120

2 * 120 = q1^2

240 = q1^2

15.49 = q1

Therefore, the charge on each coin is approximately 15.49

28. A parallel plate capacitor consists of two rectangular plates, each with an area of 4.5 cm2 and are separated from each other by a 2.00 mm thick dielectric with a dielectric constant of 5.26. The capacitor is connected to a 12.0 V battery. How much energy is stored in the capacitor?


Ec = 7.541 x 10⁻¹⁰ J

Further explanation

A capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor with a dielectric constant can be formulated :

C

=κε₀A/d

C = capacitance = Farad(F)

k = dielectric constant

ε₀ = the permittivity of free space; ε0 = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m

A = surface area, m²

d =  the distance between the plates, m

The energy stored in a capacitor (E) :

Ec=QV/2 = CV²/2 = Q²/2C

Q =the charge on a capacitor

V = voltage applied

Given

Area = A = 4.5 cm²=4.5 x 10⁻⁴ m²

Distance = d = 2 mm = 2 x 10⁻³ m

Dielectric constant=κ= 5.26

Voltage = V = 12 V

Required

The energy stored

Solution

Ec= κε₀AV²/2d

Ec = 5.26 x 8.85 × 10⁻¹² x 4.5 x 10⁻⁴ x 12²/(2 x 2 x 10⁻³)

Ec = 7.541 x 10⁻¹⁰ J

29. What is the importance of dielectric in a capacitor?


Answer:

Introducing a dielectric into a capacitor decreases the electric field, which decreases the voltage, which increases the capacitance. A capacitor with a dielectric stores the same charge as one without a dielectric, but at a lower voltage. Therefore a capacitor with a dielectric in it is more effective.
