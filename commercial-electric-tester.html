Answer:

Multimeters are absolutely necessary for any type of electrical work. From installing a ceiling fan to changing a junction box, using a multimeter helps determine if wires are hot or not (and so much more). Multimeters are designed to measure three fundamental components of electrical energy: volts, amps and ohms.

3.  What is a conductor? an insulator?

- A conductor is a material or object that allows charge to flow in one or more directions. Metals are often used as electrical conductors. An electrical insulator is a substance through which the electron does not flow freely or the insulator's atoms have closely bound electrons whose internal electric charges do not flow freely; under the influence of an electric field, very little electric current can flow through it.

4. What materials are good conductors of electricity? insulators?​

- The majority of metals are considered to be strong electrical current conductors. Copper is one of the most common materials used to produce conductors. Silver, gold, and aluminum are some of the other materials that can be used as conductors.
