15. 1. A sample of gas occupies a volume of 12.5 L at 625 mmHg pressure and 150 C? What would be the pressure be, in mmHg, if the volume was increased to 15 L and the temperature was increased to 300 C? A. 706 mmHg B. 710 mmHg C. 745 mmHg D. 760 mmHg 2. A mixture of nitrogen, oxygen, and neon gases exerts a total pressure of 893 torr. If the mole fraction of this three gases are 0.41, 0.35, and 0.24 respectively, What is the partial pressure of the nitrogen gas? A. 313 torr B. 366 torr C. 203 D. 214 torr 3. What pressure will be exerted by 0.400 mol of a gas in a 5.00 L container at 17.0 C? A. 1.95 atm B. 2.00 atm C. 1.90 atm D. 1.85 atm


Problem 1:Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{625 mmHg}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{150°C + 273 = 423 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{12.5 L}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{15 L}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{300°C + 273 = 573 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{625 mmHg} × \frac{\text{12.5 L}}{\text{15 L}} × \frac{\text{573 K}}{\text{423 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{706 mmHg}}[/tex]

Answer:

A. 706 mmHg

Problem 2:Given:

[tex]P_{T} = \text{893 torr}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{N}_{2}} = 0.41[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{O}_{2}} = 0.35[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{Ne}} = 0.24[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{\text{N}_{2}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{\text{N}_{2}} = X_{\text{N}_{2}}P_{T}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{N}_{2}} = \text{(0.41)(893 torr)}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{\text{N}_{2}} = \text{366 torr}}[/tex]

Answer:

B. 366 torr

Problem 3:Given:

[tex]V = \text{5.00 L}[/tex]

[tex]n = \text{0.400 mol}[/tex]

[tex]T = \text{17.0°C + 273.15 = 290.15 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]PV = nRT[/tex]

[tex]P = \frac{nRT}{V}[/tex]

[tex]P = \frac{(\text{0.400 mol})(0.082057 \: \frac{\text{L • atm}}{\text{K • mol}})(\text{290.15 K})}{\text{5.00 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{1.90 atm}}[/tex]

Answer:

C. 1.90 atm

21. a gas occupies 746 ml at STP. what will be its volume at 500 mmHg and 300K? (1 atm =760 mmHg)​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{760 mmHg}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{273 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{746 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{500 mmHg}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{300 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{746 mL} × \frac{\text{760 mmHg}}{\text{500 mmHg}} × \frac{\text{300 K}}{\text{273 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{1250 mL}}[/tex]

Remember:

The pressure and temperature of an ideal gas at STP are 1 atm (760 mmHg) and 273 K, respectively.
