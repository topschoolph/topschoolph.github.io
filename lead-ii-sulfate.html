10. What is the percent by mass of 5.0 g of iron (II) sulfate dissolved in 75.0g of water?​


The percent by mass : 6.25%

Further explanation  

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm) or mole fraction. The concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.  

So the concentration of a solution can be expressed as a percent which is the ratio of solute to solution. Can be expressed in percent of mass (m / m) or volume percent (v / v)  

 [tex]\tt \%mass=\dfrac{mass~solute}{mass~solution}\times 100\%[/tex]

Solution = Solute + Solvent  

The solute is the substance that is dissolved usually in smaller amounts when compared to solvents  

Given

mass solute = mass of iron (II) sulfate-FeSO₄ = 5 g

mass solvent = mass of water-H₂O = 75 g

mass solution = mass solute + mass solvent

mass solution = 5 + 75 =80 g

Required

The percent by mass

Solution

Input the value :

[tex]\tt \%mass=\dfrac{5}{80}\times 100\%\\\\\%mass=6.25\%[/tex]

=Al×2+s×32+o×12

=27×2+32×3+16×12

=54+96+192

=342.3.

Explanation:

formula mass of Al2(SO4)3 is 342.3. The relative formula mass of an ionic compound is the ratio of the average mass of one formula unit of the ionic compound to the mass of 1/12 the mass of a carbon-12 atom. Thus, to obtain the formula mass of Al2(SO4)3, just add up the relative atomic masses of every atom in one formula unit.

Thus, the relative formula mass of Al2(SO4)3 = 2*27.0 + 3*(32.1+4*16) = 342.3.

25. iron reacts with copper sulphate cuso4 and form iron (ii) sulfate (FeSO4) and copper


Fe reacts with CuSO₄ is classified as a Single replacement reaction

Further explanation

There are several possible reactions to chemical reactions

1. Synthesis 2. Decomposition 3. Single replacement 4. Double-Replacement

Fe reacts with CuSO₄ and form FeSO₄ and Cu

From The metal activity series (voltaic series)

Li-K-Ba-Ca-Na-Mg-Al-Mn- (H2O) -Zn-Cr-Fe-Cd-Co-Ni-Sn-Pb- (H) -Cu-Hg-Ag-Pt-Au

we can see that Fe is to the left of Cu so that Fe is a strong reducing agent and can push the Cu metal so that reactions occur

And this reaction is classified as a Single replacement reaction:

A + BC ---> AC + B

in which one element replaces the other elements of a compound to produce new elements and compounds

Substances involved in chemical reactions are reactants and products

The reactants in this reaction:

Fe and CuSO₄

While the reaction product :

FeSO₄ and Cu
