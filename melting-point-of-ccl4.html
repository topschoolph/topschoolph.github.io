1. The boiling point constant for CCl4 is 5.03°C, the boiling point of CCl4 is 7.70°C. Determine the boiling point of a solution made by dissolving 0.50 mole of a substance in 100.0 g of CCl4.​


The boiling point of a solution : Tb solution = 32.85 °C

Further explanation  

Solution properties are the properties of a solution that don't depend on the type of solute but only on the concentration of the solute.  

The term is used in the Solution properties  

1. molal  

that is, the number of moles of solute in 1 kg of solvent  

[tex]\tt m=\dfrac{mol~solute}{mass~1~kg~solvent}[/tex]

 

2. boiling point

Solutions from volatile substances have a higher boiling point and lower freezing points than the solvent  

[tex]\tt \Delta Tb=Kb\times m[/tex]

ΔTb = Tb solution - Tb solvent  

ΔTb = boiling point elevation  

Given

Kb CCl₄ : 5.03°C

the boiling point of CCl₄ is 7.70°C.(Tb solvent)

Required

Tb solution

Solution

molal of solution

mol solute = 0.5

mass solvent (CCl₄) = 100 g = 0.1 kg

molal of solution :

= 0.5 : 0.1 kg

= 5 m

The boiling point

Kb = 5.03 °C/m

Input the value :

Tb solution - Tb solvent = Kb x m

Tb solution - 7.70°C = 5.03 x 5

Tb solution = 32.85 °C

4. If 0.64 g of adrenaline (C9H13NO3) is dissolved in 36.0 g of CCl4 produces a boiling point elevation of 0.49 0C. What is the molality of the solution?


The molality of the solution : 0.097

Further explanation

Solution properties are the properties of a solution that don't depend on the type of solute but only on the concentration of the solute.

The term is used in the Solution properties

• 1. molal (m)

that is, the number of moles of solute in 1 kg of solvent

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{m=\dfrac{mol}{kg~solvent}}}[/tex]  

• 2. Boiling points

Solutions from non-volatile substances have a higher boiling point and lower freezing points than the solvent

ΔTb = Tb solution - Tb solvent

ΔTb = boiling point elevation

  ΔTb = Kb. m

Kb = molal boiling point increase

m = molal solution

We complete the data:

The boiling point constant for CCl(Kb) : 5.03 ° C / m

ΔTb = 0.49 ° C

Molar mass of Adrenaline: 183,204 g / mol

So there are two ways we can find molal from solution:

1. From the number of solutes (Adrenaline) and solvents (CCl₄)

[tex]\rm m=\dfrac{mol~Adrenaline}{kg~solvent(CCl_4)}\\\\m=\dfrac{0.64\div 183.204}{0.036~kg}\\\\m=\boxed{\bold{0.097}}[/tex]

2. from the boiling point formula

[tex]\rm \Delta Tb=Kb.m\\\\0.49^oC=5.03^oC/m\times m\\\\m=\dfrac{0.49}{5.03}\\\\m=\boxed{\bold{0.097}}[/tex]
