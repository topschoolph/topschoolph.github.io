15. In how many possible ways can the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd placer be declared in the final round?


Answer:

In mathematics, permutation is the act of arranging the members of a set into a sequence or order, or, if the set is already ordered, rearranging its elements is a process called permuting. Permutations differ from combinations, which are selections of some members of a set regardless of order. Permutations are studied in almost every branch of mathematics, and in many other fields of science.

Formula of Permutation:

A formula for the number of possible permutations of k objects from a set of n. This is usually written as nPk, and the formula is nP_k=  n!/(n-k)!=n(n-1)(n-2)…(n-k+1).

In how many possible ways can the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd placer be declared in the final round?

Solution:

nP_k=  n!/(n-k)!  

3P3 = 3!/ (3 – 3)!

       = 3x2x1 / 0!

Note: If we get the factorial of 0, the answer is 1.

       = 6/1

       = 6

The possible ways that the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd placer can be declared in the final round is 6 ways.

17. placer mining effects in the environment​


Answer:

Placer mining is usually aimed at removing gold from stream sediments and floodplains. Because placer mining often occurs within a streambed, it is an environmentally-destructive type of mining, releasing large quantities of sediment that can impact surface water for several miles downstream of the placer mine.


18. compare and contrast placer and in situ mining​


Answer:

Placer mining is used to sift out valuable metals from sediments in river channels, beach sands, or other environments. In-situ mining, which is primarily used in mining uranium, involves dissolving the mineral resource in place then processing it at the surface without moving rock from the ground.
