10. A school sets a fine of P30.00 for the first littering offense. The fine increases by P5.00 for each subsequent offense. •Do you think this situation illustrates a pattern of arithmetic sequence? •If so, can you illustrate the first five terms of the arithmetic sequence?


Answer:

the arithmetic sequence of that is 30,35,40,45,50

Answer:

Yes, the situation illustrates an arithmetic sequence because it has a common difference of 5 between consecutive terms.

The sequence: 30, 25, 40, 45, 50

Step-by-step explanation:

The offenses are the terms a₁, a₂, a₃, a₄, a₅.

First offense, a₁ = 30

Add 5 to the succeeding/subsequent offense:

a₂ = 30 + 5 = 35  (second offense)

a₃ = 35 + 5 = 40  (third offense)

a₄ = 40 + 5 = 45 (fourth offense)

a₅ = 45 + 5 =  50  (fifth offense)

Therefore, the first five terms representing the first five offenses are:

30, 25, 40, 45, 50

Where there is common difference (d) of 5.

Therefore, the situation illustrates an arithmetic sequence because it has a common difference of 5 between consecutive terms.


11. fine the of the 30 terms of an arithmetic sequence whose first term is 18 and a common difference of 2 .​


Answer:

18, 20, 22, 24, 26, 28, 30, 32, 34, 36, 38, 40, 42, 44, 46, 48, 50, 52, 54, 56, 58, 60, 62, 64, 66, 68, 70, 72, 74, 76

Step-by-step explanation:

Arithmetic Sequence is one of the types of sequences. The basic definition of this sequence is that the differences of every term and their preceding terms are equal on each other or constant which is what we called the common difference. Common difference was denoted as "d".

The General Term of the Arithmetic Sequence is:

[tex]a_{n} = a_{1} +(n-1)d[/tex]

Substitute the n with the numbers 1 to 30 since you are finding each term from 1st to 30th term. Then, make a table of values and list down the values under the [tex]a_{n}[/tex] which is your terms.

Example:

You want to get the 3rd term and you have the first term which is 18 and common difference of 2.

        [tex]a_{n} = a_{1} +(n-1)d[/tex]

        [tex]a_{3} = 18 +(3-1)(2)[/tex]

        [tex]a_{3} = 22[/tex]

Then insert 22 in the table and repeat the same process for the next terms.

    n          [tex]a_{n}[/tex]

    1           18

    2          20

    3          22

       

[TIP] Easiest way to define the terms using the given first term and common difference:

The easiest way is that the term will be added with the common difference then you will get the next term. Repeat the same process to define the terms needed in the sequence.

          Example: The first term is 18 and the common difference is 2.

                18,...

            18 + 2 = 20

                18, 20,...

            20 + 2 = 22

                18, 20, 22,...

If there is a given number of terms, you may try to find out the last term by using the general term of Arithmetic Sequence

        Example: Last term will be the 30th terms

          [tex]a_{n} = a_{1} +(n-1)d[/tex]

          [tex]a_{30} = a_{1} +(30-1)(2)[/tex]

          [tex]a_{30} = 76[/tex]

        Therefore, you will end the sequence if you reach 76 (30th term)

        18, 20, 22,..., 76
