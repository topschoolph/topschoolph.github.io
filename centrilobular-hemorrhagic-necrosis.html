27. What is the definition of Hemorrhagic stroke?


Answer:

Hemorrhagic stroke is a type of stroke that occurs when a blood vessel in the brain ruptures and causes bleeding in or around the brain. This can lead to brain damage and other serious neurological complications. Hemorrhagic strokes can be caused by a variety of factors, including high blood pressure, aneurysms, arteriovenous malformations, and head trauma. Treatment for hemorrhagic strokes typically involves emergency medical intervention to stop the bleeding and prevent further damage to the brain.

The cells of a multicellular organism are members of a highly organized community.
