Mr. and Mrs. bunnell purchased a house and lot worth 4 million they paid a down payment of he 800,000 they plan to amortize the loan of 3200000 by paying monthly for 20 years the interest rate is 12% convertible monthly how much is the monthly payment?

[tex]\huge{✏Answer}[/tex]

[tex]Monthly \: payment = p33,000[/tex]

[tex]Total \: interest \: paid = p7,920,000[/tex]

[tex]\huge{✏Solution}[/tex]

Given:

[tex]Loan amount (P)=p3,300,000[/tex]

[tex]Timeperiod(T)=20 \: years[/tex]

[tex]Rate \: of \: interest(R) = 12\%[/tex]

To Find:

[tex]Monthly \: payment [/tex]

[tex]Total \: interest \: paid [/tex]

Solution:

From given,

[tex]Total \: interest = \frac{PRT}{100} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{(p3,300,000)(12)(20)}{100} [/tex]

[tex] = (p33,000)(12)(20)[/tex]

[tex] = p7,920,000[/tex]

[tex]∴ monthly \: payment \: = \frac{p7,920,000}{20 \times 12} [/tex]

[tex] = p33,000[/tex]


7. Mr ribaya paid 200 000 as down payment for a car the remaining amount is to be settled by paying 16200 at the end of each month for 5 years


Answer:

Ordinary Annuity:

Cash Value:

The cash price of Ms. Ribaya's car is ₱ 953, 702. 20. To solve for this, we need to understand several concepts including down payment (d), interest, present value (P), regular payment (R), interest rate per period (j), and the number of payments (n).

Solutions:

Given: ₱ 200, 000 - down payment

₱ 16, 200 - regular payment

10.5 % - interest which is compounded monthly so we will have

.105/12 = .00875 which is the interest rate per period

5 years - time

60 - number of payments which is given by 12 months x 5 years

First, find the present value of the car using the formula: P = R 1 - (1 + j)⁻ⁿ

j

P = R 1 - (1 + j)⁻ⁿ

j

P = ₱ 16, 200 (1 - 1 + .00875)⁻⁶⁰

.00875

P = ₱ 6, 594.89425

.00875

P = ₱ 753, 702. 20

Therefore, the present value of the car is P = ₱ 753, 702. 20.

Next, find the cash value of the car.

Cash Value = Present Value + Down payment
