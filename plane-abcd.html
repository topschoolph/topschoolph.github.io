3. Let ABCD be a pyramid whose base ABCD is a square. The sides a, SA are perpendicular to the plane (ABCD) and SA = a. Calculate distance d(BC,SD)


Step-by-step explanation:

Let O be the center of the square base ABCD and M be the midpoint of SA. Since OM is perpendicular to the plane (ABCD) and SA is perpendicular to (ABCD), OM || SA. Thus, OMAD is a rectangle, and OB = OD = a/2.

Let N be the foot of the altitude from S to the base ABCD. Since SN is perpendicular to the plane (ABCD), SN || SA, and SMNS is a rectangle, with SM = NS = a/2.

Let P be the intersection of BC and SM. Since PM is perpendicular to BC, we have BP^2 + PC^2 = BC^2, by the Pythagorean theorem.

Let Q be the intersection of SD and SN. Since DN is perpendicular to SD, we have DQ^2 + QS^2 = SD^2, by the Pythagorean theorem.

Note that BP || DQ and CP || QS, since BP and DQ are both perpendicular to SM and CP and QS are both perpendicular to NS. Thus, we have BP/DQ = CP/QS, by similar triangles.

Let x = BP/DQ = CP/QS. Then BP = x DQ and CP = x QS. Substituting in the Pythagorean theorem for BP and CP, we get:

(x DQ)^2 + PC^2 = BC^2

BP^2 + (x QS)^2 = SD^2

Adding these two equations, we get:

(x^2 + 1) (DQ^2 + QS^2) + PC^2 + CP^2 = BC^2 + SD^2

But DQ^2 + QS^2 = SD^2 (since DQNS is a rectangle) and PC^2 + CP^2 = PM^2 (since P is on SM and PM is perpendicular to BC).

Thus, we have:

(x^2 + 1) SD^2 + PM^2 = BC^2 + SD^2

Simplifying, we get:

PM^2 = (x^2 - 1) SD^2 + BC^2

Note that PM is the altitude from P to BC, and SN is the altitude from S to BC. Thus, we have:

PM/SN = BP/DQ = x

Multiplying both sides by SN, we get:

PM = x SN = x (a/2) = (BP/DQ) (a/2) = (a/2) (BC/DQ)

Substituting into the previous equation, we get:

(a/2)^2 (BC/DQ)^2 = (x^2 - 1) SD^2 + BC^2

Multiplying both sides by 4/DQ^2, we get:

a^2 (BC/DQ)^2 = 4 (x^2 - 1) SD^2 + 4 BC^2/DQ^2

But a^2 (BC/DQ)^2 = a^2 (BC/SN)^2 = a^2 (BC^2 + SN^2)/SN^2 = a^2 (BC^2 + SA^2)/SA^2 = a^2 (BC^2 + a^2)/(a/2)^2 = 4 (BC^2 + a^2)

Substituting into the previous equation and simplifying, we get:

d^2 = BC^2 - 2/3 a^2

Thus, d = BC sqrt(1 - 2/3 (a/BC)^2).
