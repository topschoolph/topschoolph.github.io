27. A utility company charges $0.28 per kilowatt hour of electricity plus a $35 basic connection fee each month. Which function models an electric bill as a function of electricity usage (k)?


Answer:

Hi Khaor254,

The answer to your problem is  

[tex]f(k) = 0.28k + 35[/tex]

This kind of function is described as a linear function. Linear functions are functions whose resulting graph is a straight line. It has one dependent variable and one independent variable, Thus there is no exponent expressed on the independent variable making it simple to evaluate and easy to be sketched.

Step-by-step explanation:

Here how we solve this problem

First you need to identify what is the dependent variable and independent variable. As for our problem the independent variable is the electricity usage also represented as “k” while the electric bill will be our dependent variable that will be represented as “f(k)”.  

In essence the resulting electric bill [f(k)] depends on what will be the value of the electricity usage (k).

Next, we need to identify all factors that affects the dependent variable.  

“A utility company charges $0.28 per kilowatt hour of electricity plus a $35 basic connection fee each month.”

This statement from the problem implies that our independent variable is being processed by the integers 0.28 ($0.28 per kilowatt-hour of electricity) and 35 ($35 basic connection fee).

As said from the problem, the integer 0.28 is being multiplied to the independent variable k and the resulting product is being added to the second integer 35.

[tex]0.28k + 35[/tex]

The process of addition is implied by the word “plus” between the two integers. Thus resulting to our required function

[tex]f(k) = 0.28k + 35[/tex]
