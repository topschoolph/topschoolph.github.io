12. it is a highly selective semi-permeable border that separates the circulating blood from the brain and extracellular fluid in the central nervous system​


Answer:

The blood–brain barrier (BBB) is a highly selective semipermeable border of endothelial cells that prevents solutes in the circulating blood from non-selectively crossing into the extracellular fluid of the central nervous system where neurons reside.[1] The blood-brain barrier is formed by endothelial cells of the capillary wall, astrocyte end-feet ensheathing the capillary, and pericytes embedded in the capillary basement membrane.[2] This system allows the passage of some molecules by passive diffusion, as well as the selective transport of various nutrients, ions, organic anions, and macromolecules such as glucose, water and amino acids that are crucial to neural function.[3]

15. An object of volume 2e-6 m^3 and density 6000 kg/m^3 is immersed inside a fluid of density 5000 kg/m^3. Calculate the force exerted by the fluid on the object.


Answer:

0

Explanation:

The force exerted by the fluid on the object is the weight of the fluid displaced by the object, which is known as Archimedes' principle.

Step 1: Calculate the mass of the object using its volume and density:

mass = density x volume = 6000 kg/m^3 x 2e-6 m^3 = 0.012 kg

Step 2: Calculate the weight of the object using its mass and acceleration due to gravity, which is 9.81 m/s^2:

weight = mass x acceleration due to gravity = 0.012 kg x 9.81 m/s^2 = 0.11772 N

Step 3: Calculate the volume of fluid displaced by the object using its volume and the ratio of densities:

volume of fluid displaced = volume of object x (density of object / density of fluid)

= 2e-6 m^3 x (6000 kg/m^3 / 5000 kg/m^3) = 2.4e-6 m^3

Step 4: Calculate the weight of the fluid displaced using its mass and acceleration due to gravity:

weight of fluid displaced = mass of fluid displaced x acceleration due to gravity

= density of fluid x volume of fluid displaced x acceleration due to gravity

= 5000 kg/m^3 x 2.4e-6 m^3 x 9.81 m/s^2 = 0.11772 N

Step 5: Compare the weight of the object to the weight of the fluid displaced:

Since the weight of the fluid displaced is equal to the weight of the object, the net force on the object is zero. Therefore, the fluid exerts a force of 0 N on the object.

The extracellular fluid, in particular the interstitial fluid, constitutes the body's internal environment that bathes all of the cells in the body. 28. An object of volume 25e-5 m^3 and density 525 kg/m^3 is floating in a fluid with a density of 1250 kg/m^3. Calculate the volume of the object exposed above the surface of the fluid.


Answer:

weight = density x volume x gravity

where density is the density of the object, volume is the volume of the object, and gravity is the acceleration due to gravity, which we'll take as 9.81 m/s^2.

weight = 525 kg/m^3 x 25e-5 m^3 x 9.81 m/s^2

weight = 0.129 mN

we can find the buoyant force:

buoyant force = density of fluid x volume of displaced fluid x gravity

The volume of the displaced fluid is equal to the volume of the object that is submerged in the fluid, which we'll call V_submerged.

buoyant force = 1250 kg/m^3 x V_submerged x 9.81 m/s^2

Since the object is floating, the buoyant force is equal to the weight of the object

buoyant force = weight

1250 kg/m^3 x V_submerged x 9.81 m/s^2 = 0.129 mN

Solving for V_submerged, we get:

V_submerged = 1.06e-5 m^3

The volume of the object exposed above the surface of the fluid is equal to the total volume of the object minus the submerged volume

V_exposed = volume - V_submerged

V_exposed = 25e-5 m^3 - 1.06e-5 m^3

V_exposed = 23.94e-5 m^3

Therefore, the volume of the object exposed above the surface of the fluid is 23.94e-5 m^3.
