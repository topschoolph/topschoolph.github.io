How would you describe the noble gases?

The noble gases are colourless, odourless, tasteless, nonflammable gases.

What are the elements under noble gases?

Group 8A (or VIIIA) of the periodic table are the noble gases or inert gases: helium (He), neon (Ne), argon (Ar), krypton (Kr), xenon (Xe), and radon (Rn). The name comes from the fact that these elements are virtually unreactive towards other elements or compounds.

20. What Korean song is being referred to as"Music of Noble Class"?​


Answer:

Explanation:

The two major divisions of traditional Korean music are commonly transcribed as chong-ak and sog-ak.

Jeongak or Chong-ak means "right or proper music". Its tradition includes both instrumental and vocal music, which was developed by the upper class literati of Joseon society.

29. what a reflection about keiji nishitani's quote each thing is itself itself in not being itself and is not itself in being itself being illusion in its truth and truth in its illusion​


Answer:

As problematic and convoluted as it may seem, what Nishitani tries to portray is an image of utter meaninglessness of existence. This quote briefly shows us a glimpse into Nishitani's philosophical works. A belief that is similar to Existentialists like Kierkegaard. On the surface level, it is quite easy to reflect on his philosophical standpoints as it is, a form of philosophical inquiry that explores the problem of human existence and centers on the lived experience of the thinking, feeling, acting individual. However, I, myself would want to reflect deeper into the quote, back to its origin. Nishitani's works are parallel to later western works of the same topic. Nishitani, proudly presents how Sunyata is similar to the western concept of Nihilism. This short history is what captivated me on Nishitani's works, it shows me that even though we learn and study a lot more about foreign works, there should at least be somethings from my own culture that I can relate them to. Not to present our country for the things we bring but to help me understand the foreign teachings better to contextualize them into my own local situation.
