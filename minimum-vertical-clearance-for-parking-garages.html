13. Jim spent $13 to park for 5 hours. The parking garage charges a base rate of $4 for the first two 2 hours, and then an hourly rate for each additional hour, h. The equation 3h + 4 = 13 models Jim's parking cost. How much does each additional hour of parking cost?


Here, we are already given that the equation 3h + 4 = 13 models Jim's parking cost, where h is the number of additional hours. What we have here is a linear equation of the form y = mx + b.

When we are given equations of this form, we say that :  

b is the y-intercept, this means that when x = 0, y = b, and  m is the slope, this means that for every unit (by one) increase in the x-value, the y-value increases by m units.

In particular, for the given example:

x is h, the number of additional hours, y is 13, the parking cost at h additional hours, b, the y-intercept, is 4 (we know this since it's the constant part of the model) m, the slope, is 3 (we know this since it's the coefficient of x, or in this case, h)

Based on the slope, this means that for each additional hour (x), the parking cost (y) increases by 3 (m). So the final answer is $3.

It also makes sense if you look at it from this angle:

Jim parked for 5 hours, right? Now, it is given that the $4 flat rate is for 2 hours. This means that when there are no additional hours (h = 0), he is charged $4. This also means that he is charged 5 - 2 = 3 additional hours. We know from the given that for 5 hours, he was charged $13. This means that he was charged $13 for 3 additional hours.

So now, we have two points (0, 4) and (3, 13), where each (x, y) pair is the number of additional hours, and the total parking cost respectively.  

To get the slope, we first subtract the pairs of x's and y's:

x pair: 3 - 0 = 3 y pair: 13 - 4 = 9

Then, we divide the difference in y's, by the difference in x's:

9/3 = $3, which is the same answer we got just by looking at the slope of the equation.

Here's a tip! In general, that is how to compute for slopes, using two points of the same line, we just use: (change in y) / (change in x).

By the way, we can also check that (3,13) is on the equation, so:

3h + 4 = 13

By substituting h = 3 into the equation, we get:

3(3) + 4 = 13

By simplifying the left hand side, we get:

9 + 4 = 13

13 = 13, which is true.

In a similar way, we can check that (0,4) is on the equation as well. We use 3h + 4 = 4 instead of the given 3h + 4 =13 because the y-value we are testing now is 4, and not 13. So, again, by substituting h = 0, we get:

3(0) + 4 = 4

4 = 4, correct.

22. A ramp in Parking garage rises to connect two levels of Parking space. The two levels are parallel with each other. The ramp makes 15 with the lower horizontal what angle does the ramp make with the upper horizontal?​


Answer:

Since the two levels of parking space are parallel, the ramp forms a right triangle with the lower horizontal and the hypotenuse being the ramp itself. The angle between the ramp and the lower horizontal is 15 degrees.

Let's call the angle between the ramp and the upper horizontal "x". Since the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees, we know that the third angle in the right triangle formed by the ramp, lower horizontal, and hypotenuse is 90 degrees. Therefore, we can use the fact that the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees to find x:

x + 90 + 15 = 180

Simplifying this equation gives:

x = 75

Therefore, the angle between the ramp and the upper horizontal is 75 degrees.
