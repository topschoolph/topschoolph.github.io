8. Guide Questions:1. What was John's intention in writing his gospels?2. How is John's gospel different from the Synoptics?3. Who are the intended reader of his gospels?4. How was he called by Jesus?5. What is his symbol?​


John clearly stated the purpose of his Gospel, “These are written that you may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that believing you may have life in His name” (Jn. 20:31). John presented Christ so persuasively that his readers believe and receive eternal life through Him.The Gospel of John is unique from the “synoptic Gospels” (Matthew, Mark, and Luke), so-called due to their similar content. The Synoptics cover many of the same miracles, parables, and events of Jesus’ life and ministry. There is a lot of overlap, repetition, and even some parallel passages that are nearly identical. Generally speaking, the Synoptics tell us what Jesus said and did; John tells us who Jesus is. The Synoptics focus on the signs and sayings of Christ; John emphasizes the identity of Christ.It is quite evident that John's gospel is aimed at the Jewish community, especially when Jesus said, “I am the living bread which came down from heaven.” This fact was completely contradicted by many of the Jewish communities especially the nobles including the high Pharisees. John was the son of Zebedee, a Galilean fisherman, and Salome. John and his brother St. James were among the first disciples called by Jesus. In The Gospel According to Mark, he is always mentioned after James and was no doubt the younger brother. His mother was among those women who ministered to the circle of disciples. James and John were called Boanerges, or “sons of thunder,” by Jesus, perhaps because of some character trait such as the zeal exemplified in Mark 9:38 and Luke 9:54, when John and James wanted to call down fire from heaven to punish the Samaritan towns that did not accept Jesus. John and his brother, together with St. Peter, formed an inner nucleus of intimate disciples. In the Fourth Gospel, ascribed by early tradition to John and known formally as The Gospel According to John, the sons of Zebedee are mentioned only once, as being at the shores of the Sea of Tiberias when the risen Lord appeared. Whether the “disciple whom Jesus loved” (who is never named) mentioned in this Gospel is to be identified with John (also not named) is not clear from the text.Eagle. In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression and basic mathematics specially in solving unknown variables.

Given:

9 pieces of notebook

4 pieces of ballpen

each ballpen costs Php 18

he paid at most Php 500

Solution:

Let x – notebook, and

y – ballpen

Cost = price(notebook) + price(ballpen)

= x(9) + 18(4)

9x + 72 ≤ 500

9x ≤ 500 - 72

9x ≤ 428

x ≤ 438/9

x ≤ 47.56

How much is the notebook?

Since the condition of inequality is at most (less than of equal) do not round off 47.56 to 48.

At most means less than or equal to
