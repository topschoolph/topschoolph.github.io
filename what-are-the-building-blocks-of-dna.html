2. what are the building blocks of DNA


DNA is made up of six smaller molecules -- a five carbon sugar calleddeoxyribose, a phosphate molecule and four different nitrogenous bases (adenine, thymine, cytosine and guanine).


The two DNA strands are termed polynucleotides since they are composed of simpler monomer units called nucleotides. Each nucleotide is composed of one of four nitrogen-containing nucleobases — cytosine (C), guanine (G), adenine (A), or thymine (T) — a sugar called deoxyribose, and a phosphate group.

14.) THE SUGAR FOR DNA IS CALLED

THE PENTOSE SUGAR IN DNA IS CALLED (DEOXYRIBOSE) , AND IN RNA, THE SUGAR IS RIBOSE.

THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN THE SUGARS IS THE PRESENCE OF THE HYDROXYL GROUP ON THE 2' CARBON OF THE RIBOSE AND ITS ABSENCE ON THE 2' CARBON OF THE DEOXYRIBOSE.

ANSWER:

(DEOXYRIBOSE)
