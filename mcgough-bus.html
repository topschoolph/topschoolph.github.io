v1 = 50 kph ; v2 = ? kph ; t1 = 2 h ; t2 = 1 h

Let v1 = the speed of bus 1 and let v2 = the speed of bus 2

Let t1 = the travel time of bus 1 and let t2 = the travel time of bus 2

distance = speed * time

distance = v1 * t1 = 50 kph * 2 h = 100 km

9. The bus departs the bus terminal every 2 hours. If the first bus is scheduled to depart at 10:35 a.m., when should the second bus depart?​


PROBLEM:

The bus departs the bus terminal every 2 hours. If the first bus is scheduled to depart at 10:35 a.m., when should the second bus depart?

SOLUTION:

• If the bus departs the terminal every two hours and the first scheduled time is at 10:35 a.m, then the second bus depart will be two hours after.

• Thus, from 10:35 a.m, you need to add two hours.

[tex]\large\boxed{ \tt 10:35+2:00=12:35}[/tex]

ANSWER:

• At exactly 12:35 in the afternoon (p.m), the bus will depart for the second time.

Answer:

The 1st bus left the station and traveled for two hours, and the speed rate of the bus is 50kph.

Let S1 be the speed of the first bus, and S2 be the speed of 2nd bus.

Let's solve the distance traveled by the 1st bus:

Distance = Speed * time

Distance = S1 * Time = 50kph * 2h = 100kph

The second bus overtook the first bus at the time that the first bus traveled 100 km.

Let's solve the speed of the second bus:

2nd bus = distance/travel of time

              = 100 km/1 hour

Thus, the rate of the second bus is 100km per hour (100km/h)
