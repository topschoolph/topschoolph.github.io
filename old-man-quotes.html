11. explain the quote "a man is a measure of all things"​


The quote "a man is a measure of all things" means that humans are the standard by which all things are judged.

The quote, attributed to the ancient Greek philosopher Protagoras, reflects the belief that humans are the ultimate source of truth and knowledge. According to this perspective, all things are measured and evaluated based on how they relate to human needs, values, and experiences. The quote emphasizes the central role of humans in shaping their own perceptions of reality and asserts that human judgment is the ultimate standard for evaluating the world around us.

Answer:

According to Aristotle, "Man is by nature a political animal." This indicates that he is destined to live as a social political person by his own nature, because he is governed by a natural desire to exist in the company of other men, he is a being by which political activity is innately possible.
