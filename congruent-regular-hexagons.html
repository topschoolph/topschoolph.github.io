Which is true about the sides of a regular hexagon inscribed in circle?

c. the side of the regular hexagon is congruent to the radius of the circle

A regular hexagon inscribed in a circle will form an isosceles triangle with its 2 sides equal to the radius of circle.

Being an isosceles, that means it's two base angles must be equal, so it actually forms an equilateral triangle now and not just an isosceles triangle.

Now that it's an equilateral triangle, then all sides are equal, so the 2 radius and the side length of the hexagon are all equal.

Hence we prove that the side of the regular hexagon is congruent to the radius of the circle

22. construch a regular hexagon​


Answer:

Place your compass point on the paper and draw a circle. (Keep this compass span!)

2. Place a dot, labeled P, anywhere on the circumference of the circle to act as a starting point.

3. Without changing the span on the compass, place the compass point on P and swing a small arc crossing the circumference of the circle.

4. Without changing the span on the compass, move the compass point to the intersection of the previous arc and the circumference and make another small arc on the circumference of the circle.

5. Keep repeating this process of "stepping" around the circle until you return to point P.

6. Starting at P, connect to each arc on the circle forming the regular hexagon.
