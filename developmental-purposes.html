4. developmental journalism​


Answer:

Development journalism was conceived in the 1960s at the Press Foundation of Asia (PFA), where Filipino journalists Alan Chalkley and Juan Mercado were concerned that news organizations were inadequately covering socio-economic development. Journalists were reporting government press releases and quotes but giving little attention to detailed analysis, interpretation, or evaluation of development projects, policies, or problems. Their concerns led Chalkley and Mercado to organize seminars at the PFA to train journalists in the art of development journalism

10. differentiate john santrocks in developmental stages from robert havighurst six developmental stage​


Answer:

The development stages cited by Santrock are the same with Havighurst's six (6) developmental stages, however, Havighurst did not include the prenatal period. Havighurst combined infancy and early childhood while Santrock mention them as two (2) separate stages.

Explanation:

According to John Santrock (2011), the human lifespan is divided into eight periods: prenatal period (conception to birth), infancy (birth to 18-24 months), early childhood (2-5 years), middle and late childhood (6-11 years), adolescence (10-12 to 18-21 years), early adulthood (20s to 30s), middle adulthood (40s to 50s), and late adulthood (60s – 70s to death).

On the other hand, Havighurst's developmental task model includes six stages of life: infancy and early childhood from birth to age 5, middle childhood between ages 6 and 12, adolescence between ages 13 and 18, early adulthood between ages 19 and 30, middle adulthood from age 30 to age 60, and later maturity, which is after age 60.

12. How do santrock developmental stages compare to havighurst's developmental stages? ​


Child Development

Santrock's list of developmental phases is identical to Havighurst's six developmental stages, with the exception that Havighurst omitted the prenatal stage. While Santrock refers to early childhood and infancy as two distinct stages, Havighurst integrated the two.

A kid passes through numerous phases of human development psychologically from birth to maturity. The ability to hold up one's head, the ability to communicate, and the ability to play are just a few of the developmental milestones that may help you determine whether or not your kid is developing at the appropriate rate.

One of child psychology's main interests is these milestones. Child psychology specialists frequently categorize developmental changes in a child's growth. They are able to explain why these changes occur and how a child's surroundings and culture effect these changes via the use of these divisions.

Santrock’s Developmental Stages

John Santrock's phases of development, which depict a person's complete life, are one of the more recent development theories in the area of psychology. According to Santrock's thesis, a person's life is divided into eight phases, including:

prenatal stage (infancy to birth)Infancy (birth to 18-24 months) (birth to 18-24 months)earliest years childhood (2-5 years)Middle and late childhood (6-11 years)Adolescenceyouthful adulthooda middle-aged adultlater in life

Havighurst’s Developmental Tasks Theory

According to Havighurst's view, people must complete one developmental stage before going on to the next. A person is encouraged to grow and feel more accomplished by peers and supportive communities. The phases of Havighurst's theory are as follows, along with the developmental activities they correspond to:

Early childhood (birth to five years): learning to walk, talk, and engage in social interactionsMiddle childhood (ages 6 to 12): Developing literacy skills and increasing independenceTeenagers (ages 13 to 17): Educated on gender-based responsibilitiesEarly Adulthood (18-35): Developing a profession and starting a familyAge between 36 and 60: Getting used to physiological changesLater Maturity (over 60): Getting used to retirement and old age.

22. before developmental biology was used, what was the term used , why it change to developmental biology​


Answer:

The term used prior to the development of developmental biology was embryology. This term was used because embryology is the study of the development of an embryo. With the development of modern biology, the term developmental biology emerged as it covered the entire process from fertilization to the formation of adults. This term encompasses not only the study of the development of an organism from its inception, but also the process of maturation, growth and differentiation of cells.
