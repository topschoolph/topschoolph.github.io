6. A. heat flows from one object to another by the movement of particles from hot region to cold region; B. radiation; C. heat is a thermal energy that flows from one object to another; D. heating a metal spoon in boiling water, walking barefoot in concrete or sand during summer and the heat energy from your one's hand transferred another hand; E. convection always involves the circulation of a liquid or gas 1. What is heat? 2. Examples that show the transfer of heat through conduction? 3. What is convection? 4. Heat transfer that does not need any contact between the heat source and the heated object? 5. How does heat flows from one object to another?​


Answer:

1. Heat is a form of energy that is transferred from one object to another due to a difference in temperature. It is a form of thermal energy that flows from a hotter object to a cooler object.

2. Examples that show the transfer of heat through conduction include heating a metal spoon in boiling water, touching a hot stove, or holding a hot cup of coffee. In each of these examples, heat is transferred from a hotter object to a cooler object through direct contact.

3. Convection is the transfer of heat through the movement of fluids, such as liquids or gases. This occurs when a fluid is heated, causing it to become less dense and rise, while cooler fluid sinks to take its place. This creates a circular motion that transfers heat from one part of the fluid to another.

4. Radiation is the heat transfer that does not need any contact between the heat source and the heated object. It occurs when heat energy is emitted in the form of electromagnetic waves, such as light or infrared radiation. Examples of radiation include the heat from the sun or a fire.

5. Heat flows from one object to another by the movement of particles from a hot region to a cold region. This can occur through conduction, convection, or radiation. In conduction, heat flows through direct contact between two objects. In convection, heat flows through the movement of fluids. In radiation, heat flows through the emission of electromagnetic waves.
