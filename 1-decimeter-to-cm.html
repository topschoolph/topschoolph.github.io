Answer:

To find the combined area of the rectangle and the semi-circle, we need to first find the area of each figure and then add them together.

The area of the rectangle is length times width, so

Area of rectangle = 7 ft x 4 ft = 28 sq ft

The diameter of the semi-circle is the same as the width of the rectangle, which is 4 feet. The radius of the semi-circle is therefore half of the diameter, or 2 feet.

The area of a semi-circle is half of the area of a full circle with the same radius, so

Area of semi-circle = 1/2 x pi x (2 ft)^2 = 2 pi sq ft

Adding the area of the rectangle and the semi-circle, we get:

Combined area = 28 sq ft + 2 pi sq ft

Using a calculator, we can find an approximate value for the combined area:

Combined area ≈ 34.28 sq ft

Therefore, the combined area of the rectangle and the semi-circle is approximately 34.28 square feet.

The area of the rectangular board is 6 decimeters x 4 decimeters = 24 square decimeters.

The circular picture has a diameter of 4 decimeters, which means its radius is 2 decimeters.

The area of a circle is pi times the radius squared, so

Area of circle = pi x (2 dm)^2 = 4 pi sq dm

To find the area of the board that can be seen, we need to subtract the area of the circular picture from the area of the rectangular board:

Area of board that can be seen = 24 sq dm - 4 pi sq dm ≈ 11.31 sq dm

Therefore, the area of the board that can be seen is approximately 11.31 square decimeters.

The photograph measures 20 cm by 12 cm, so its area is:

Area of photograph = 20 cm x 12 cm = 240 sq cm

The margin around the photograph is 2 cm on all sides, which means the cardboard must be 4 cm longer and 4 cm wider than the photograph to accommodate the margin.

The dimensions of the cardboard are therefore:

Length = 20 cm + 2 cm + 2 cm = 24 cm

Width = 12 cm + 2 cm + 2 cm = 16 cm

The area of the cardboard is length times width, so

Area of cardboard = 24 cm x 16 cm = 384 sq cm

To find the area of the cardboard that is not covered by the photograph, we need to subtract the area of the photograph from the area of the cardboard:

Area not covered by photograph = 384 sq cm - 240 sq cm = 144 sq cm

Therefore, the area of the cardboard that is not covered by the photograph is 144 square centimeters

30. We have a string with a lenghth of 2.3 cm. How many centimeters should we cut off the string, so that there would be 1 decimeter left?


Conversion Factor (C.F.):

10 cm = 1 dm


Given:

2.3 cm to 1 dm.


Equation:

(2.3 cm) x (1 dm / 10 cm)


Solution:

(2.3 cm) x (1 dm / 10 cm) = 0.23 dm


100 - 23 = 77 cm = 0.77 dm


You need 77 cm more to have 1 decimeter.
