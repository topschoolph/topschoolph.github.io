13. WHAT ARE THE DIFFERENT CLASSIFICATION OF COMPOUNDS? 70-80 words​


Chemical compounds may be classified according to several different criteria. One common method is based on the specific elements present. For example, oxides contain one or more oxygen atoms, hydrides contain one or more hydrogen atoms, and halides contain one or more halogen (Group 17) atoms. Organic compounds are characterized as those compounds with a backbone of carbon atoms, and all the remaining compounds are classified as inorganic. As the name suggests, organometallic compounds are organic compounds bonded to metal atoms.

Another classification scheme for chemical compounds is based on the types of bonds that the compound contains. Ionic compounds contain ions and are held together by the attractive forces among the oppositely charged ions. Common salt (sodium chloride) is one of the best-known ionic compounds. Molecular compounds contain discrete molecules, which are held together by sharing electrons (covalent bonding). Examples are water, which contains H2O molecules; methane, which contains CH4 molecules; and hydrogen fluoride, which contains HF molecules.

The Reproductive Health (RH) Bill, also known as the Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Act of 2012, is a Philippine legislation that aims to provide universal access to reproductive health services, including family planning, maternal care, and sex education.

The RH Bill has been controversial in the Philippines, with some groups opposing it on religious and moral grounds. However, its supporters argue that the bill is necessary to address the country's high maternal mortality rate, high incidence of teenage pregnancy, and widespread poverty.

One of the key provisions of the RH Bill is the promotion of family planning services, including the provision of contraceptives, to enable couples to make informed decisions about their reproductive health. The bill also aims to provide better access to maternal care services, including pre-natal and post-natal care, to reduce the incidence of maternal deaths.

Another critical aspect of the RH Bill is providing comprehensive sex education in schools, which aims to equip young people with accurate information about reproductive health and responsible sexual behavior. This is seen as a crucial step in reducing the incidence of teenage pregnancy, which is a significant problem in the Philippines.

Opponents of the RH Bill argue it goes against traditional Filipino values and promotes promiscuity and immorality. However, supporters say that the bill is not promoting promiscuity but rather promoting responsible behavior and providing people with the tools to make informed decisions about their reproductive health.

Overall, the RH Bill is a significant step towards addressing the reproductive health needs of Filipinos, especially those who are marginalized and disadvantaged. 28. what is an electron? explain! 70 to 120 words


[tex]________________________________[/tex]

electronAn electron is a negatively charged subatomic particle that can be either bound to an atom or free (not bound).What is an electron?

An electron is a negatively charged subatomic particle that can be either bound to an atom or free (not bound). An electron that is bound to an atom is one of the three primary types of particles within the atom -- the other two are protons and neutrons.

Together, electrons, protons and neutrons form an atom's nucleus. A proton has a positive charge that counters the electron's negative charge. When an atom has the same number of protons and electrons, it is in a neutral state.

Electrons are unique from the other particles in multiple ways. They exist outside of the nucleus, are significantly smaller in mass and exhibit both wave-like and particle-like characteristics. An electron is also an elementary particle, which means that it is not made up of smaller components. Protons and neutrons are thought to be made up of quarks, so they are not elementary particles.

Shells, subshells and orbitals

In the early days of atomic study, scientists believed that an atom's electrons circled the nucleus in spherical orbits at specific distances, much like planets circle a sun. In this model -- referred to as the Bohr model -- the orbits furthest from the nucleus contain the greatest amount of energy. When an electron jumps from a higher energy orbit to a lower energy orbit, the atom releases electromagnetic radiation.
