Some misconceptions and inadequate notions about the Holy Eucharist include seeing it as just a symbol or representation, not recognizing the true presence of Christ in the Eucharist, and not understanding the transformative power of receiving the Eucharist.

The points discussed in the chapter help correct these imbalances by emphasizing the reality of Christ's presence in the Eucharist, the significance of the Eucharist as a sacrament, and the importance of receiving the Eucharist worthily. . 'Good Friday' comes from the obsolete sense 'pious, holy' of the word "good". Less common examples of expressions based on this obsolete sense of "good" include "the good book" for the Bible, "good tide" for "Christmas" or Shrovetide, and Good Wednesday for the Wednesday in Holy Week.
