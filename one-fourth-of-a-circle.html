Answer:

because diameter is equal to 2r

Answer:

C. One-half

Step-by-step explanation:

The radius of a circle is the length of the line from the center to any point on its edge. On the other hand, the diameter is the length of the line through the center that touches two points on the edge of the circle. One of the formulas for the diameter of a circle is d = 2r. One of the formulas for the radius of a circle, on the other hand, is r = d/2. So basically, the length of the radius is one-half the length of a diameter of a circle.

✒️SECTOR AREA

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\: \rm 19.625 \: sq. \: cm. [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Determine the area of the sector with the given formula.

[tex] \rm A_{Sector} = \frac{m\overset{\frown}{PQ}}{360} \, \big(\pi r^2\big) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm A_{Sector} = \frac{90}{360} \, \big(\pi (5)^2\big) \: cm^2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm A_{Sector} = \frac{90}{360} \, \big(\pi (5)^2\big) \: cm^2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm A_{Sector} = \frac{\,1\,}{4} \, \big(25\pi \big) \: cm^2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm A_{Sector} = \frac{\,25\pi\,}{4} \: cm^2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm A_{Sector} = 6.25\pi \:cm^2\\ [/tex]

Let 3.14 be the approximate value of pi.

[tex] A_{Sector} \approx 6.25(3.14) \: cm^2 [/tex]

[tex] A_{Sector} \approx 19.625 \: cm^2 [/tex]

Therefore, the area of the sector PTQ is approximately 19.625 sq. centimeters

20. the radius of a circle has endpoints (4, -1) and (8, 2). what is the equation that defines the circle if its center is at the fourth quadrant?


✏️CIRCLE EQUATION

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

The radius of the circle has endpoints (4,-1) and (8,2). What is the equation that defines the circle if its center is at the fourth quadrant?

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad\large\rm» \:\: \green{(x - 4)^{2} + (y + 1)^{2} = 32} [/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

» If the center is at the fourth quadrant, it's x-coordinate is positive and it's y-coordinate is negative.

[tex] \rm Quadrant IV = ( + \ , - ) [/tex]

» From the given endpoints, the center must be (4,-1). Now, the equation of the circle in standard form is written as:

[tex] \rm (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 [/tex]

» Where (h,k) is the center and r is the radius. Substitute the given center to the equation.

[tex](x - 4)^{2} + [y - ( \text - 1)]^{2} = {r}^{2} [/tex]

[tex](x - 4)^{2} + (y + 1)^{2} = {r}^{2} [/tex]

» Find the square of the radius by substituting one of its endpoints is at (8,2).

[tex](8 - 4)^{2} + (3 + 1)^{2} = {r}^{2} [/tex]

[tex](4)^{2} + (4)^{2} = {r}^{2} [/tex]

[tex]16 + 16 = {r}^{2} [/tex]

[tex]32 = {r}^{2} [/tex]

» The square of the radius is 32, then substitute it to the equation with the given center.

[tex](x - 4)^{2} + (y + 1)^{2} = 32[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] (x - 4)² + (y + 1)² = 50 is the standard form of the equation.
