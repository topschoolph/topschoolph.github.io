22. What is the molality of an aqueous KBr solution with a mole fraction of KBr, X, equal to 0.245?


Molality

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm), or mole fraction. Concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.  

Molality shows how many moles are dissolved in every 1000 grams of solvent. The molality of an aqueous KBr solution: 18,027 m.

Further explanation

Molality can be formulated:

[tex]\tt molality(m)=\dfrac{mol~solute}{kg~solvent}=\dfrac{mol~solute\times 1000}{mass~solvent(g)}[/tex]

[tex]\tt m=n\times \dfrac{1000}{p}[/tex]

[tex]\tt m=\dfrac{mol}{kg~solvent}[/tex]

m = Molality  

n = number of moles of solute  

p = solvent mass (grams)  

Given

a mole fraction of KBr: 0.245

Required

The molality of an aqueous KBr solution

Solution

mol KBr = 0.245, so mol of water (solvent) = 0.755

mass of water:

[tex]\tt =mol\times MW\\\\=0.755\times 18~g/mol\\\\=13.59~g\\\\=0.01359~kg[/tex]

molality of solution:

[tex]\tt m=\dfrac{mol~solute}{mass~solvent(kg)}\\\\m=\dfrac{0.245}{0.01359~kg}\\\\m=\boxed{\bold{18.027~m\rightarrow 18.0~m}}[/tex]

29. Problem: What volume of a 1.50.M KBr solution contains 66.09 KBr?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to use the relationship between the concentration of the solution, the amount of solute present in the solution, and the volume of the solution.

The equation that relates these variables is:

amount of solute = concentration × volume

We can rearrange this equation to solve for the volume:

volume = amount of solute ÷ concentration

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

volume = 66.09 g ÷ 1.50 mol/L

Using the periodic table, we can find the molar mass of KBr, which is 119 g/mol.

To convert the amount of KBr from grams to moles, we divide by the molar mass:

66.09 g ÷ 119 g/mol = 0.555 mol

Substituting this value into the equation for volume, we get:

volume = 0.555 mol ÷ 1.50 mol/L = 0.370 L

Therefore, 0.370 L or 370 mL of the 1.50 M KBr solution contains 66.09 g of KBr.
