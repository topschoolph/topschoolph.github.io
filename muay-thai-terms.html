People:

Thais and Filipinos are both friendly, but I believe the latter is more open to strangers than the former. Also, most Thais are gentler and more reserved, whereas Filipinos are more outgoing. Both enjoy partying and drinking, and once the alcohol kicks in, they will definitely let their hair down.

culture:                                                                                                                      Very distinct. Food:

Thai cuisine is known for its use of a lot of chili peppers and relies heavily on herbs and spices. Regional dishes exist, but their food is generally fiery and spicy. Filipino food is rich in pork and seafood, and almost every dish is flavored with vinegar and other souring agents such as calamansi, citrus, and sour fruits. Influences range from Malay to Polynesian, from Indian to Chinese, and from Spanish to American.

Language:

Thai is a tonal language based on ancient languages such as Khmer and Sanskrit. They have their own writing system, as opposed to the Philippines, which has adopted the Western alphabet. More Filipinos than Thais can speak, read, and write English, and the Filipino language, like the food, has a variety of influences.

There are two traditional forms of theater in Thailand: khon and lakhon. The khon form began in the 15th century, and it was aimed mostly to delight the royal court. Also lakhon was originally performed only inside the palace, but it is characterized by slower and more graceful movements.

The only contrast in culture is that the Philippines were colonized by the Spaniard for 300+ years. Which is why Catholicism and Christianity are the religions dominated in their population. However, Thailand was never colonized by Spaniard or any European country.  It was the only country in Southeast Asia that wasn't colonized. Because of this, Thailand and Philippines have different religions as well as structure of government and leadership in their country. The heads of government are different.

1. They are popular for their beaches. Beaches are their tourist attraction.
