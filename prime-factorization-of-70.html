Finding the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The greatest common factor or GCF refers to the highest factor that the numbers have in common. It is the highest number that can divide the numbers evenly. There are three methods to find the greatest common factor or GCF of numbers: listing method, prime factorization method and continuous division method. Let us identify each of them.

Listing Method - This is the method where you have to write or list down the factors of the numbers. Then, find the highest number that they have in common.

Prime Factorization Method - This is where you have to break down the numbers into their factors until you end up with their prime numbers.

Continuous Division Method - In this method, you have to write the given numbers in a row separated by commas, then divide the numbers by a common prime number. You will stop dividing after reaching the prime numbers.

✏ANSWER

TRUE

Step-by-step explanation:

In the prime factorization method, we can only factorize a number into its prime factors. Prime numbers are the numbers that have only two factors, 1 and the number itself. For example, 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, and so on. Prime factors of 70 are 2, 5, and 7.

How to Calculate the Factors of 70?

Factors of any number n can be calculated by many methods; one of the methods is dividing the number by the smallest of the factors.

Factors of number 70 can be calculated as follows:

Step 1: Write the smallest two factors of 70 (except for 1). The smallest two factors of 70 are 2 and 5.Step 2: Divide 70 by 2 and 5.70/2 = 35 and 70/5 = 14So, 2, 5, 14, and 35 are the factors of 70.

Step 3: Now take the next factor of 70.The next factor of 70 is 7.Divide 70 by 7.70/7 = 10So, 2, 5, 7, 10, 14, and 35 are the factors of 70.Step 4: Include 1 and the number itself to the factors.

Hence, the factors of 70 are 1, 2, 5, 7, 10, 14, 35, and 70.
