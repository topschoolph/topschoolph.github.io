1. Marking lining tools


LINING TOOLS

⇛LINING TOOLS are tools used to mark what is measured or to be measured. Some lining tools are pencil, chalkline, marking gauge, mortise gauge, and awl.

PENCIL ➦ In the past, carpenter's pencil is a requirement in woodworking. It has the same length as an ordinary pencil; however, it's lead and body are flat and bigger.

CHALKLINE ➦ This is boxlike instrument with a hallow pat wherein a spool for string and a small piece of rug are inserted. When the string is pulled out, it passes through the rug which is wet with pulverized charcoal. As both ends of the string are tightly fixed on the desired length to be measured, the string is plucked toward the surface.

MARKING GAUGE ➦ Most common of all gauges is the marking gauge. It consists of a wooden bar called beam about 8 inches (20.3 cm) long wherein wooden block or head slides.

MORTISE GAUGE ➦ This is a marking gauge with two spurs nailed in two beams held together inside the head. Both beams are adjustable and can be spaced at different distances to mark two parallel lines simultaneously.

AWL ➦ This is a pointed tool used for making location of nail and screw. It is also used for scratching lines especially on metal sheets of plants called Scratch or badawl, the tool looks like an ice pick.

18. what tool is used in marking lines in sheet metal?​


Answer:

DIVIDERS

Dividers are used to mark out shapes onto sheet metal. They are used just like compasses to scrape circles and arcs onto the metal. They work best if a small indent is placed on the sheet metal using a centre punch for one of the legs to rest in.
