Answer:

Rock paper scissors is a hand game usually played between two people, in which each player simultaneously forms one of three shapes with an outstretched hand. These shapes are "rock", "paper", and "scissors".

Step-by-step explanation:

To explain this, say rock crushes scissors (no need to actually crush). If one player picks scissors and the other paper, the player who showed scissors succeeds. Scissors cuts paper. If a player shows paper while the other shows rock, the player who picked paper succeeds.

26. What is the probability of getting 5 ties in a row in a game of rock, paper, scissors?​


Answer:

At the Rock Paper Scissors International World Championships we would regularly see strings of 5 or 6 stalemates (throws repeated by both players). I personally refereed a match that went as high as 10. I have heard stories of matches that involved up to 13 in a row, but not sure how likely that is. 100? Not a chance, so long as both players are not colluding with each other.

We would likely have replaced the ref at least 3 times

Both players would likely begin developing swelling on their wrists and the fleshy area between thumb and index finger due to repetitive strain. This would make a throw of Scissors quite painful
