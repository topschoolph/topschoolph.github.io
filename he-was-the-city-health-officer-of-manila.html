The recent news article about the Manila Mayor's visit to Bataan caught my attention. It was heartwarming to see how the mayor, despite being from another province, showed great interest and concern for the welfare of Bataan and its people.

Mayor Isko Moreno's visit was more than just a courtesy call to the governor; it was a testament to the strong bond he shares with Bataan. Having relatives in Pilar town, he affirmed that he is a "Bataeno at heart." This showed not just his fondness for the place but also his respect for its people.

The exchange of ideas and best practices between the chief executives was a powerful move to fight against COVID-19 and its impact on livelihood and the economy. I was impressed by the health measures implemented by Mayor Moreno in Manila, such as the establishment of quarantine facilities and renovating hospitals, to ensure efficient public hospital operations. One key takeaway from their conversation was that being a true public servant is not just about leading from the front, but it's about how the critical leadership values and competencies that are necessary to become highly effective agents of change.

It was heartening to hear Mayor Moreno reminisce about his younger days when he used to work at a farm in Pilar town. This shows that he values his past experiences and recognizes how crucial they are in shaping one's perspective in life.

Moreover, when he expressed his aim to enter into a sisterhood agreement with the Municipality of Pilar once the COVID-19 crisis resolves, it showed how keen he is to strengthen the ties between Manila and Bataan.

Overall, Mayor Isko Moreno's visit to Bataan highlighted the importance of interprovincial connectivity and cooperation. It was inspiring to see two local chief executives joining hands to fight a common battle against COVID-19. What are transitional devices?

Cohesive devices are another name for transitional words and phrases. They improve the writing's flow and make the connections between ideas, phrases, and paragraphs more clear. Transitional elements act as links between different sections of your paper. The reader can interpret the concepts that a paper develops with the use of these cues. Words or phrases that serve as transitional devices help move thought from one sentence to the next, from one idea to another, or from one paragraph to the next.

Conjunctive adverbs and conjunctions are the two main categories of transitions. A referent is the name given to another form of transition. The relationships between sentences are made clearer by the use of transitional words and phrases. Depending on their purposes, transitions can be categorized into several classes.
