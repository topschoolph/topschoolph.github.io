5. ___2. Choose the correct sequence of events in cellular respiration a.Krebs --> ETC --> glycolysis b. Krebs cycle --> glycolysis--> ETC c. glycolysis--> fermentation --> Krebs cycle d. glycolysis --> Krebs cycle --> ETC


Answer:

The correct option is d. Glycolysis, Krebs cycle, electron transport

Explanation:

Pyruvate is obtained as the terminal product of glycolysis. Aerobic respiration, in oxygen presence, uses this pyruvate in the Krebs cycle to produce more energy as ATP. After the event of the Krebs cycle, the passage of molecules takes place through the ETC (electron transport chain), which leads to the generation of a proton gradient, which contributes to ATP synthesis.
