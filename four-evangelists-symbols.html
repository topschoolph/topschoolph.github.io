2. How do the symbols for the evangelists help you appreciate the four Gospels?​


Answer:

What are the 4 Gospels and their symbols?

Matthew (winged Man) Mark (winged Lion) Luke (winged Ox) John (eagle)

How do the symbols for the evangelists help you appreciate the four Gospels?

This is actually something that is not original to Scripture, but was an early church history meditation on the Gospels. The earliest Christian that that I am aware of who expressed this meditation was Irenaeus (Irenaeus was a Greek bishop known for guiding and expanding Christian communities). Irenaeus wrote something about these symbolisms and concluded that the Gospels are in accord with these things, among which Jesus Christ is seated.

These images are not comprehensive descriptions of each Gospel, but they do express an element of truth. Matthew does present Christ as the royal king of His people. Mark presents Christ as the sacrifice for sin. Luke presents Christ as truly man. John presents Jesus was the sender of the Holy Spirit.

The Lion of Saint Mark, representing Mark the Evangelist, pictured in the form of a winged lion, is an aspect of the Tetramorph. On the pinnacle of St Mark's Cathedral he is depicted as holding a Bible, and surmounting a golden lion which is the symbol of the city of Venice and formerly of the Venetian Republic.

Luke the Evangelist, the author of the third gospel account (and the Acts of the Apostles), is symbolized by a winged ox or bull – a figure of sacrifice, service and strength. ... The ox signifies that Christians should be prepared to sacrifice themselves in following Christ.

The Eagle of Saint John (Spanish: Águila de San Juan) is a heraldic eagle associated mostly with the Catholic Monarchs which was later prominently used during Francoist Spain (1939–77) and the Spanish transition to democracy (1977–81). It is sable with an or halo and feet of gules.
