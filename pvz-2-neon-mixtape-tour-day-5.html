Solving the problem of linear equations of one variable and the inequality of one variable.What is a linear equation?

A linear equation is an equation that has a minimum of a ranked variable with the highest degree of the variable being one. Why is it called linear? Because the relationship of this mathematical relationship is depicted with a straight line in a cartesian coordinate system.

One Type of Linear Equations in One Variable Linear Equation

As the name implies, a one-variable linear equation contains only one variable of rank one.

The general forms of the One Variable Linear Equation are:

ax + b = 0

where:

a = coefficient

b = constant

x = variable

One-variable linear inequality?

A single-variable linear inequality is a form of inequality having a single variable or modifier in which this inequality is usually represented by symbols <, >, ≤ and ≥. Inequality is always marked with a hyphen < to express less than, > to express more ≤ than or less than or equal to, as well as ≥ to express not less than or more than or equal to.

The Case: A van rental charges P5,500.00 flat rate for a whole-day tour in CALABARZON of 5 passengers and each additional passenger added P500.00 to the tour fare. Express a piecewise to show to represent the van rental in terms of a number of passengers n.​

Given: Van rental charges for 5 passengers is P5500.

Find: Express a piecewise function to show to represent the van rental in terms number of passengers n.

Solution:

given that, van rental charges for 5 passengers is P5500.

So,

A number of passengers = less than or equal to 5.

Total charge = P5500 .

Then, 5500 for n ≤ 5.

Now, given that, each additional passenger added P500 to the tour fare.

and, Total passengers are n.
Total charge = charges for 5 passengers + charges for more than 5 members with each paying P500.
Total passengers = n.

Therefore, total charge = 5500 + 500(n - 5) where n > 5.

Hence, required piecewise function of total charge = 5500 + 500(n - 5) where n > 5.

=> 5500 + 500n - 2500 where n > 5 .

=> 3000 + 500n where n > 5. (Ans.)

So, piecewise functions are:-

5500 n ≤ 5.3000 + 500n n > 5.

18. C. Answer the following questions: 1. What is a tour? 2. What is tour guiding? 3. What are the roles that tour guides play during a tour? 4. Cite 5 (five) characteristics of a tour guide 5. What are the limitations of a tour guide? 6. How to become a professional tour guide? 7. What is a tour guide's client? 8. What are the operational problems of a tour guide during a tour? 9. What are the elements of a tour guide? 10. What is the meaning of the Greek word tornos?​


Answer:

1. A tour is a planned and organized trip, usually for leisure or educational purposes, that involves visiting different places of interest.

2. Tour guiding is the profession of providing assistance, information, and interpretation to people who are visiting places of interest.

3. The roles that tour guides play during a tour include:

- Providing information about the destination, its history, culture, and attractions

- Ensuring the safety and comfort of the tourists

- Managing the itinerary and logistics of the tour

- Facilitating communication and interaction between the tourists and locals

- Creating a fun and engaging experience for the tourists

4. Characteristics of a tour guide include:

- Knowledgeable about the destination

- Good communication skills

- Flexibility and adaptability

- Personable and friendly

- Organized and detail-oriented

5. Limitations of a tour guide include:

- Language barriers

- Limited time at each attraction

- Limited flexibility in the itinerary

- Unpredictable weather or other unforeseen circumstances

6. To become a professional tour guide, one usually needs to complete a training program or obtain a certification from a recognized organization, such as a tourism board or association.

7. A tour guide's client is typically a tourist or group of tourists who are visiting a destination and have hired a tour guide to assist them.

8. Operational problems of a tour guide during a tour can include issues with transportation, accommodations, weather, and unexpected events such as strikes or closures of attractions.

9. The elements of a tour guide include:

- Knowledge about the destination and its attractions

- Good communication skills

- Management of logistics and itinerary

- Interpersonal skills and ability to connect with tourists

- Flexibility and problem-solving skills

10. The Greek word tornos means "to turn around," which may refer to the idea of a guide leading a group of tourists around a destination.
