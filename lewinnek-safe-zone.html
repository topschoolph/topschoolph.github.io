4. there is a circular hole to traveled animals whose equation is x^2 + y^2 minus 6x + 8y + 9 = 0. The hiker is an or coordinate (5,2). Is he in a safe zone?​


✒️CIRCLE INEQUALITY

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \:\: \rm The \: hiker \: is \: safe [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

To determine if he is safe, he must be on the outside the circle's radius. That means, we can say that:

[tex] (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 > r^2 [/tex]

[tex] x^2 + y^2 + Ax + Bx + C > 0 [/tex]

Check if (5, 2) is on the outside (safe) the circle or it is in the inside (not safe) the circle.

[tex] x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 8y + 9 > 0 [/tex]

[tex] 5^2 + 2^2 - 6(5) + 8(2) + 9 > 0 [/tex]

[tex] 10 + 4 - 30 + 16 + 9 > 0 [/tex]

[tex] 14 - 14 + 9 > 0 [/tex]

[tex] 9 > 0 [/tex]

Since the inequality is true, that means (5,2) was on the outside of the circle and we can say that the hiker is in the safe zone.
