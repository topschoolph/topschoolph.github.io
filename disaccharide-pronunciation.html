5. function of disaccharides​


Answer:

The Function of Disaccharides

In your body, a disaccharide function is to provide your body with a quick source of energy. Because they're only made up of two sugar molecules, they're easily broken down by enzymes in your digestive system into their respective monosaccharides and then absorbed into your bloodstream.

20. How disaccharide form ?


A disaccharide is produced by joining 2 monosaccharide (or single sugar) units. An example is Maltose. maltose is made up of two glucose molecules. The two monosaccharides are bonded using a dehydration reaction, which leads to the loss of a molecule of water and formation of a glycosidic bond.
