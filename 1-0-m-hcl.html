1. What volume of 4.50 m hcl can be made by mixing 5.65 m hcl with 250.0 ml of 3.55 m hcl?


[tex]\bold{Problem:}[/tex]

What volume of 4.50 M HCl can be made by mixing 5.65 M HCl with 250.0 mL of 3.55 M HCl?

[tex]\bold{Solution:}[/tex]

Here is the first way to solve this problem:

M₁ V₁ + M₂V₂ = M3V3

(3.55) (0.250) + (5.65) (x) = (4.50) (0.250 + X)

Where x is volume of 5.65 M HCl that is added

(0.250+ x) is total resultant volume

0.8875 +5.65x = 1.125 + 4.50 x 1.15x = 0.2375

X= 0.2065 L

Total amount of 4.50 M HCl is then (0.250

+ 0.2065) = 0.4565 L

Total amount = 456.5 mL

Here is the second way to solve this problem:

Since the amount of 5.65 M added is not asked for, there is no need to solve for it.

M₁ V₁ + M₂V₂ = M3V3

(3.55) (250) + (5.65) (x − 250) = (4.50) (x)

That way, x is the answer you want, the

final volume of the solution, rather than

x being the amount of 5.65 M solution

that is added.

6. Describe how you will prepare a 100-mL 0.768 M HCl solution from a stock solution of 12.0 M HCl.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: List the given values.

Note that the key word for the initial concentration is "stock solution."

[tex]\begin{aligned} & M_{\text{i}} = 12.0 \: M \\ & M_{\text{f}} = 0.768 \: M \\ & V_{\text{f}} = \text{100 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the volume of the stock solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{i}} & = \frac{M_{\text{f}}V_{\text{f}}}{M_{\text{i}}} \\ & = \frac{(0.768 \: M)(\text{100 mL})}{12.0 \: M} \\ & = \boxed{\text{6.40 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\begin{array}{l} \text{Thus, we need to dilute 6.40 mL of} \\ \text{the 12.0 M HCl solution with sufficient} \\ \text{water to give a final volume of 100 mL} \\ \text{a 100-mL volumetric flask to obtain the} \\ \text{desired concentration.} \end{array}}[/tex]

9. What is the ph of a 0.0075 m solution of hcl?


SOLUTION:

The hydrogen or hydronium concentration of a strong monoprotic acid at equilibrium is equal to the initial concentration of the acid.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{H}^+] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (0.0075) \\ & = \boxed{2.12} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pH of a 0.0075 M solution of HCl is 2.12.

[H+] = [HCl] = 0.050 M

17. What volume of 12.0 M HCl is required to make 75.0 mL of 3.50 M HCl? a) 21.9 mL b) 0.560 mL c) 257 mL d) 75.0 mL


Answer:

From the choices, the answer is letter A, 21.9 mL.

Explanation:

This problem is an application of the dilution equation in chemistry.

Dilution

Dilution is defined as the process of lowering the concentration of a solute in solution. This process can simply be done by mixing the solution with more solvent.

Solute is the substance in a solution that is being dissolved, while the solvent in a solution is the substance that is used to dissolve the solute.

The dilution equation of solutions is as follows:

C₁V₁ = C₂V₂

where

C₁ is the initial concentration of the solutionV₁ is the initial volume of the solutionC₂ is the final concentration of the solutionV₂ is the final volume of the solutionMolarity

The usual unit of the concentration of a solution is molarity. It is defined to be the moles of solute per liters of solution. In mathematical variable form, molarity is M = [tex]\frac{n}{L}[/tex].

Solving for the problem using the dilution equation, we have,

C₁V₁ = C₂V₂C₁ = 12.0 MC₂ = 3.50 MV₂ = 75.0 mL

V₁ = [tex]\frac{(C2)*(V2)}{C1}[/tex]V₁ = [tex]\frac{(3.50 M)*(75.0 mL)}{12.0 M}[/tex]V₁ = 21.875 mLV₁ = 21.9 mL (following scientific notation, round to the nearest tenths)

Therefore, the volume of the 12.0 M HCl required is 21.9 mL.

18. How many milliliters of 6.10 M HCl solution are needed to obtain 0.100 mol of HCl ?


Answer:

To calculate the volume of a solution required to obtain a certain amount of solute, we can use the equation:

Volume (in liters) = Amount of solute (in moles) / Concentration (in moles per liter)

First, we need to convert the amount of HCl from moles to millimoles:

0.100 mol HCl = 100 mmol HCl

Then we can use the above equation to calculate the required volume:

Volume = 100 mmol / 6.10 mol/L

Volume = 16.39 mL

Therefore, we need 16.39 milliliters of 6.10 M HCl solution to obtain 0.100 mol of HCl.


19. What is the concentration of H+ in a 2.5 M HCl solution?


Answer:

The concentration of H+ ions in a 2.5 M HCl solution can be calculated using the molarity of the solution and the acid dissociation constant for HCl. The acid dissociation constant for HCl is known to be equal to 1 x 10^-7.

The concentration of H+ ions in a 2.5 M HCl solution can be calculated as follows:

HCl -> H+ + Cl-

The initial concentration of HCl is 2.5 M, and the acid dissociation constant for HCl is 1 x 10^-7. This means that the concentration of H+ ions in the solution is equal to the initial concentration of HCl multiplied by the acid dissociation constant, or 2.5 M * 1 x 10^-7 = 2.5 x 10^-7 M.

Thus, the concentration of H+ ions in a 2.5 M HCl solution is 2.5 x 10^-7 M.

25. the molality of a 37.5 % (m/m) HCl.


Solution:

Step 1: Determine the mass of solute and mass of solvent.

Since 37.5% by mass HCl solution contains 37.5 g of HCl (solute) and 62.5 g of water (solvent), this can be written as

mass of solute = 37.5 g

mass of solvent = 62.5 g = 0.0625 kg

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of solute (HCl).

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 36.458 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{37.5 g}}{\text{36.458 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 1.02864 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (in kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{1.02864 mol}}{\text{0.0625 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 16.46 \: m}[/tex]
