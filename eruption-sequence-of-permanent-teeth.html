8. In a full set of permanent teeth 1/4 are incisors 1/4 are premolars and 3/8 are molars, what fraction of all the teeth are incisors premolars and molars


Answer:

The incisors, premolars and molars are equivalent to 7/8 of the wholeset of permanent teeth.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the whole set of fraction, we need to add all the given fraction given which is part of the full set of permanent teeth.

The full set of permanent teeth will be our whole or such that when you add  all the parts of the teeth given in fraction form, the result will be 1.

In the question, the given are as follows:

1/4 incisors

1/4 premolars

3/8 molars

lets first determine the least common multiple of the denominators 4, 4 and 8. It can be easily observed that the LCM is 8. That way our Least common denominator would be 8.

We need to reduce the above given fractions by first dividing the denominator of the fraction to the LCD then multiplying the quotient to the numerator.

See below:

[tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{3}{8}[/tex]   = [tex]\frac{2 + 2 +3 }{8}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{7}{8}[/tex]

Therefore the incisors, premolars and molars are equivalent to 7/8 of the whole set of permanent teeth.

21. A mutation is a permanent change in the dna sequence of a gene


Answer:

A gene variant is a permanent change in the DNA sequence that makes up a gene. This type of genetic change used to be known as a gene mutation, but because changes in DNA do not always cause disease, it is thought that gene variant is a more accurate term.

A gene mutation is a permanent alteration in the DNA sequence that makes up a gene, such that the sequence differs from what is found in most people. Mutations range in size; they can affect anywhere from a single DNA building block (base pair) to a large segment of a chromosome that includes multiple genes

While mutations always change the DNA sequence, they do not always cause a change in the resulting protein or an obvious effect on the organism. This can occur because most amino acids can be coded by two or more different codons.

27. the stage of life where the permanent teeth grows​


When kids reach around 5 or 6, their jaws are strong enough to support permanent teeth, and so baby teeth start to fall out and visits from the Tooth Fairy begin. Developing permanent teeth eventually put enough pressure on the roots of the baby teeth, which causes them to break. By the time to tooth falls out, most of the root has already been reabsorbed into the gums. Their new adult tooth could appear in as soon as a week, or as long as six months.
