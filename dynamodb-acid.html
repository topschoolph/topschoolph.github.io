7. When was direct-current (dc) dynamo invented?


When was direct-current (dc) dynamo invented?

It was 1827 when the direct-current dynamo was being experimented on by a scientist.

Anyos Jedlik, a Hungarian, began experimenting with electromagnetic rotating devices such as dynamo, which he named electromagnetic self-rotors, in 1827, independently of Faraday. Both the stationary and spinning portions of the single-pole electric starter prototype were electromagnetic.

He conceived the dynamo concept in 1856, six years before Siemens and Wheatstone, but he did not patent it since he believed he was not the first to realize it. His dynamo employed two electromagnets positioned opposite each other to produce the magnetic field surrounding the rotor instead of permanent magnets. It was also the discovery of the dynamo self-excitation, which superseded permanent magnet designs.
