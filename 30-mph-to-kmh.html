9. A car with an initial velocity of 60 mph needs 144 feet to come to a complete stop. Determine the stopping distance of this same car with an initial velocity of 30 mph


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

A car with an initial velocity of 60 mph needs 144 feet to come to a complete stop. Determine the stopping distance of this same car with an initial velocity of 30 mph

Note:

We need to solve first the deceleration on the first scenario and used the same deceleration on the main question of the problem which is (Determine the stopping distance of this same car with an initial velocity of 30 mph).

We also need to convert mph to m/s and feet to meter.

60 miles/hr × 1609.34 m/1 mile × 1 hr/ 3600 s = 26.82 m/s

30 miles/hr × 1609.34 m/1 mile × 1 hr/ 3600 s = 13.41 m/s

144 ft × 0.3048 m / 1 ft = 43.89 meter

Given Data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 60 miles/hr or 26.82 m/s

Vf (final velocity) = 0 m/s (comes to stop)

distance = 144 feet or 43.89 meter

a (acceleration) = ? unknown

Solution:

a = - (Vi)² / 2d

  = - (26.82 m/s)² / 2(43.89 m)

  = - 719.3124 m²/s² / 87.78 m

a = - 8.19 m/s² (decelerating)

Solving for stopping distance in 30 mph or 13.41 m/s initial velocity

Formula ( d = -(Vi)² / 2a )

d = -(Vi)² / 2a

  = -(13.41 m/s)² / 2 ×  - 8.19 m/s²

  =  - 179.8281 m²/s²  / - 16.38 m/s²

  = 10.98 m

Answer:

Stopping distance is 10.98 m or 36.02 feet
