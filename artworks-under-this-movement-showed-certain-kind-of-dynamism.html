27. BLOOMING, 1949 H.R OCAMPO1.Does this artwork make you feel something?what is it?2.under what art movement does this painting belong? in your own word, why do you think that this artwork is part of that specific art movement ?


Answer:

Strange as it may sound, I generally feel antsy and filled with nervous energy. Whenever I look at someone else’s art, it makes me want to do my own. I feel like I should be doing rather than looking. I tend to be almost jittery when I go to art museums or gallery openings. I don’t really know anyone else who feels that way – at least no one has ever said it — but I feel either inspired by the art or jealous that I didn’t do it. I sometimes think it’s a bit more of the latter than the former because I tend to be very analytical (not critical in the negative sense) of the artwork. I start to look at the structure and, if I can get close enough to it, the brushstrokes or movements of a pen or pencil on the paper that actually form the piece. I then think of all the things I should be doing artistically. I think that is why communities of artists flourish—they feed off of each other in a symbiotic way.

Problem is that once I leave and go back to my regular world of to-do lists and deadlines, I no longer feel as inspired and don’t actually do any art work!
