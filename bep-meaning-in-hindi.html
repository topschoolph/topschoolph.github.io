12. Problem Solving: Required for STE, optional for BEP. Show your complete solution Two vehicles are in motion. Vehicle A is 500kg moving at 10m/s. Vehicle B is 1000kg moving at 5m/s. Which vehicle has greater kinetic energy?​


Answer:

Two vehicles are in motion. Vehicle A is 500kg moving at 10m/s. Vehicle B is 1000kg moving at 5m/s. Which vehicle has greater kinetic energy?​

Given:

Vehicle A:

mass, ma = 500kg

velocity, va = 10m/s

Vehicle B:

mass, mb = 1000kg

velocity, vb = 5m/s

Required:
Kinetic Energy, KE = ?

Equation:
KE = 1/2 mv^2

Solution:

Vehicle A:

KEa = 1/2 (500 kg)(10 m/s)^2

KEa = 25,000 Joules

Vehicle B:

KEb = 1/2 (1000 kg)(5 m/s)^2

KEb = 12,500 Joules

Vehicle A has greater kinetic energy.

18. A.Show the bond of the ff compounds 1.) BeP ( Be-2 ; P-6 ) ANSWER:


Answer:

1.) BeP

The bond in BeP is a covalent bond. Covalent bonds form when atoms share electrons in order to achieve a stable electron configuration.

In BeP, the bond is formed between the beryllium atom (Be), which has two valence electrons, and the phosphorus atom (P), which has six valence electrons. In order to achieve a stable electron configuration, the atoms share their valence electrons, forming a covalent bond. The resulting compound has a total of eight valence electrons, with four coming from each atom.
