5. -Briefly discuss the importance of strategic management in businessSerious answer please, on your own not from Google ​


Strategic management is the process of developing and implementing strategies that help organizations achieve their goals and objectives. It involves analyzing the organization's internal and external environment, identifying opportunities and threats, setting goals and objectives, formulating strategies, and implementing and monitoring the strategies to ensure success.

The importance of strategic management in business cannot be overstated. It helps organizations to stay competitive in the market by ensuring that they have a clear direction and purpose. It also helps to align the efforts of employees and departments towards a common goal, which increases efficiency and effectiveness. Additionally, strategic management allows organizations to anticipate and respond to changes in the business environment, such as changes in technology, customer preferences, or market trends.

Effective strategic management also helps organizations to manage risk and uncertainty. By conducting a thorough analysis of the environment and identifying potential threats and opportunities, organizations can develop strategies that mitigate risks and capitalize on opportunities. This allows them to make informed decisions and take calculated risks that can lead to long-term success.

Overall, strategic management is essential for businesses that want to achieve sustainable growth and success. It provides a framework for decision-making, goal-setting, and performance measurement, and helps organizations to stay competitive and adaptable in an ever-changing business environment.

The source of Google's competitive advantage is the database infrastructure that has been programmed by Google for many years, the relevance of search results and product offerings from AD words to social networking sites to mobile application platforms being the main source of Google's competitive advantage.

2. What value does Google create for customers and advertisers?

Google delivers Google Ads by aggregating and anonymizing data for ecommerce advertisers to identify profiles of customers who frequently buy or visit certain products based on network traffic. Google is also making it easier to see customer value by age, gender, or audience characteristics

Google uses a generic strategy. This strategy involves a wide market scope. The company will offer products to everyone around the world. A generic competitive differentiation strategy involves developing capabilities that create a competitive business atmosphere.

25. Can you explain the concept of quantum entanglement and how it relates to the phenomenon of non-locality in quantum mechanics?*no googling do not copy the answer in the google answer on your own​


Answer:

Quantum Entanglement & Non-Locality.

Explanation:

Quantum entanglement is a phenomenon that occurs when two or more particles become correlated in a way that their quantum states cannot be described independently of each other. In other words, the state of one particle is dependent on the state of the other(s), even if they are separated by large distances.

For example, let's say we have two entangled particles, A and B. When the state of particle A is measured, the state of particle B will instantaneously "collapse" to a corresponding state, regardless of the distance between them. This instantaneously changing state of one particle based on the state of the other is what is referred to as non-locality.

Non-locality is a fundamental aspect of quantum mechanics, and it challenges our classical understanding of the physical world. In classical physics, two particles separated by a distance can only influence each other through a local interaction, such as the exchange of energy or force. However, in quantum mechanics, particles can become entangled, and their states become intertwined in a non-local way.

One of the most famous examples of quantum entanglement and non-locality is the thought experiment known as the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen (EPR) paradox. The EPR paradox suggests that if two particles become entangled, the measurement of one particle can instantly determine the state of the other, regardless of the distance between them. This idea was famously criticized by Albert Einstein, who referred to it as "spooky action at a distance."

In conclusion, quantum entanglement and non-locality are two closely related phenomena in quantum mechanics. Entanglement occurs when two or more particles become correlated in a way that their states cannot be described independently of each other. Non-locality refers to the instantaneous changing of the state of one particle based on the state of the other, regardless of the distance between them. These phenomena challenge our classical understanding of the physical world and have significant implications for the future of quantum technologies.
