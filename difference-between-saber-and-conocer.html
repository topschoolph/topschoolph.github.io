Saber teeth is a homoplastic character for Thylacosmilus and Smilodon. Homoplastic characters evolve independently in multiple taxa in different evolutionary lineages in response to adaptation in which these characters are not present in their common ancestor.

9. fossils of saber-toothed tigers show that these animals have vey large sharp.what does tell you about saber-toothed tigers?​


What Is a Sabertooth?

The "saber-toothed tiger," Smilodon, is the California State Fossil and the second most common fossil mammal found in the La Brea tar pits. The name "saber-toothed tiger" is misleading as these animals are not closely related to tigers. Juvenile to adult-sized fossils are represented in the large Berkeley collections.
