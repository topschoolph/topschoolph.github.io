17. Why is it important as a student to learn about exponential notation and order of operation? Cite an example where you can use exponential notation and order of operation.


Answer:

It is important for students to learn about exponential notation and order of operation because they are essential concepts in algebra and math more generally. Exponential notation is a way of representing repeated multiplication, and it allows us to write numbers that are very large or very small in a compact and convenient form. Order of operation is a set of rules that dictate the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed in order to get the correct result.

One example where you might use exponential notation and order of operation is when working with scientific notation. For example, suppose you are working with a number like 0.000 000 001. In scientific notation, this number would be written as 1 x 10^-9. Here, the exponent -9 indicates that the decimal point should be moved 9 places to the left, giving us the number 0.000 000 001.

In terms of order of operation, suppose you are given the expression 8 + 3 x 4. In order to correctly evaluate this expression, you would need to remember the order of operation rules and first perform the multiplication of 3 x 4, which gives 12, and then add 8 to get the final result of 20.

Overall, learning about exponential notation and order of operation is important because it allows us to write and manipulate numbers in a way that is precise and easy to understand. These concepts are also crucial for solving many types of mathematical problems, including those in science, engineering, and other fields.

Answer:

The formula for exponential population growth is dN/ dt = rN. In this equation d is the rate of change, N is the number of existing individuals, r is the intrinsic growth rate, t is time, and dN/dt is the rate of change in population size.
