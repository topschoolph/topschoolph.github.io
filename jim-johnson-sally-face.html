9. Three friends start a business and decide tosplit the profits equally. Jim invests $2,000,Mary invests $3,000, and Sally invests$5,000. If the profits are $3,000, how muchmore money would Sally make if the profitswere divided proportional to how much eachfriend invested?​


Sally would make $1,500 more money.

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio Word Problem

The problem is asking about the amount of money that Sally would make if the profits were divided proportionally to how much they invested. There are three steps to find the answer.

Give the ratio of how much they invested.Add the parts of the ratio and divide the profits by the sum.Multiply the answer by each part of the ratio.

Given:

Jim - $2,000

Mary - $3,000

Sally - $5,000

profits - $3,000

Let us now find the answer by following the steps above.

Step 1: Ratio

Express the ratio of how much they invested as Jim:Mary:Sally. Plug in the amount that each of them invested.

2,000:3,000:5,000

You can still simply this by dividing each part by a common factor which is 1,000.

2:3:5

Step 2: Add and Divide

Add the parts of the ratio to get 10.

2:3:5 = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10

Now, divide the profits by 10.

$3,000 ÷ 10 = $300

Step 3: Multiply

Multiply $300 by each part to know how much more money they would make from the profits.

Jim = 2 × $300 = $600

Mary = 3 × $300 = $900

Sally = 5 × $300 = $1,500

Final Answer:

Sally would make $1,500 more money.

28. Three friends start a business and decide to split the profits equally. Jim invests $2,000, Mary invests $3,000, and Sally invests $5,000. If the profits are $3,000, how much of the profits would Sally receive if the profits were divided proportional to how much each friend invested?


Answer:

Jim = $600Mary = $900Sally = $1500

Step-by-step explanation:

Total investment = $2000 + $3000 + $5000 = $10000

Jim = 2000 / 10000 = 0.2 or 20%

Mary = 3000 / 10000 = 0.3 or 30%

Sally = 5000 / 10000 = 0.5 or 50%

Thus, if the profit is 3000 each member will receive:

Jim = 3000 * 0.2 = $600

Mary = 3000 * 0.3 = $900

Sally = 3000 * 0.5 = $1500
