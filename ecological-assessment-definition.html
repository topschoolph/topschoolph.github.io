Answer:

The term “habitat” has several meanings. In ecology it means either the area and resources used by a particular species (the habitat of a species) or an assemblage of animals and plants together with their abiotic environment

An ecological community consists of various organisms in an area. From a theoretical systems viewpoint, an ecological community is the aggregate of organisms in groups that eat one another and that are eaten by one another, and it is a trophic structure made up of trophic compartments (food chain and food web).

3. what is the ecological definition of biome?​

7. Define Ecological Succession into 3 different definitions.


1. Ecological succession refers to the process by which an ecosystem changes and develops over time due to a series of natural disturbances or human intervention.

2. Ecological succession can also be defined as the gradual replacement of one community of plants and animals with another community in a particular area over time.

3. Another definition of ecological succession is the predictable and orderly process of community development in a given area, starting from the pioneer species to climax community.

9. Anthropocentric is? Ecocentric is? Deep ecology is? Social ecology is? Ecofeminism is?


Answer:

Anthropocentric

regarding humankind as the central or most important element of existence, especially as opposed to God or animals.

Ecocentric

focused on the environment.

Deep ecology

Deep ecology, environmental philosophy and social movement based in the belief that humans must radically change their relationship to nature from one that values nature solely for its usefulness to human beings to one that recognizes that nature has an inherent value.

Social Ecology

Social ecology is the study of how individuals interact with and respond to the environment around them, and how these interactions affect society and the environment as a whole.

Ecofeminism

Ecofeminism, also called ecological feminism, branch of feminism that examines the connections between women and nature.

A way an animal's body helps it survive, or live, in it's environment.______5. The way an organism interacts with biotic and abiotic factors of the environment.______​


Explanation:

1.Definition: A system that includes all living organisms (biotic factors) in an area as well as its physical environment (abiotic factors) functioning together as a unit.

2.An ecosystem is made up of all the living animals and plants and the non-living matter in a particular place, like a forest or lake. ... It is obvious that all forms of life in the ecosystem are dependent on all other living and non-living things for food, nutrients and energy

3.An abiotic factor is a non-living part of an ecosystem that shapes its environment. In a terrestrial ecosystem, examples might include temperature, light, and water. In a marine ecosystem, abiotic factors would include salinity and ocean

4.An adaptation is a way an animal's body helps it survive, or live, in its environment. Camels have learned to adapt (or change) so that they can survive. Animals depend on their physical features to help them obtain food, keep safe, build homes, withstand weather, and attract mates.

5.Ecology is the study of how organisms interact with one another and with their physical environment. The distribution and abundance of organisms on Earth is shaped by both biotic, living-organism-related, and abiotic, nonliving or physical, factors.
