6. Which events take place in the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis? Light energy is converted sugars; sugars are broken down for energy. Light energy is captured by plants; light energy is used to make sugar. Light energy is converted to chemical energy; chemical energy is used to make sugar. Light energy is captured by plants; light energy is converted to chemical energy.


Answer:

Light energy is captured by plants; light energy is converted to chemical energy.

Explanation:

During the light dependent reactions of photosynthesis, photons from the light energy coming from the sun is captured by the light absorbing molecules in the thylakoid of the chloroplast. These captured energy is passed from one receptor to another an excites the electron or gives a higher state of energy from its ground state. The energized electron is transferred to an electron acceptor and is replaced by an electron from H2O. As this happens, water splits into 2 Hydrogen ions and 1/2 Oxygen. As Hydrogen ions are released to the outside to the lumen or inner membrane of the thylakoid against a concentration gradient, these ions aid in the formation of chemical energy in the form of ATP, a phosphorylated form of Adenosine Diphosphate by chemiosmosis and the reduction of coenzyme nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate (NADP+) to NADPH.

To summarize the things occuring in the light dependent reactions:

1. Energy absorbed from the sunlight

2. Splitting of water molecules

3. Hydrogen ions are transported and build up

4. NADPH production

5. Diffusion of hydrogen ions

6. ATP production

Synthesis of sugar using the chemical energy produced during the light dependent reactions are used during the light independent reactions or Calvin Cycle of photosynthesis. During this process, carbon dioxide is added, three-carbon molecules are formed, exit, and recycled.
