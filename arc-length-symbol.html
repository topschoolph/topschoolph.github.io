11. Find the length of an arc of a circle if the diameter is 24cm and the arc measures 90°?​


✏️ARC LENGTH===============================

Problem: Find the length of an arc of a circle if the diameter is 24 cm and the arc measures 90°?

Solution: Find the length of the arc in which it is the product of the ratio of the angle subtended to it at 360 degrees, and the circle's circumference.

[tex]\begin{aligned}& \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula: } \\ & \boxed{ \ell = \frac{ \theta}{360 \degree} \cdot2\pi r } \end{aligned}[/tex]

- The diameter of the circle is twice its radius, thus we can say that the circumference of the circle is the product of its diameter and pi.

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ \ell = \frac{90 \degree}{360 \degree} \cdot2\pi(24cm) } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ \ell = \frac{1}{4} \cdot\pi(48cm) } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ \ell = \frac{\pi(48cm)}{4} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ \ell = \pi(12cm)}\end{aligned}[/tex]

- Let 3.14 be the approximate value of pi.

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ \ell≈ (3.14)(12cm)}\end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ \ell≈ 37.68cm}\end{aligned}[/tex]

- Therefore, the length of the arc is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 37.68 \: cm \: }}[/tex]===============================

27. if an arc of a circle is 60° and the radius is 10cm, what is the length of an arc?​


We can use the formula s = r*theta to solve for the arc length. To use this formula, the angle theta needs to be in radians. 60 degrees in radians is equivalent to π/3 radians. R is given to us to be 10 cm. Now we can solve for s, the arc length.

s = r*theta = 10cm * (π/3) = 10π/3 cm, so answer C

Explanation:

We can also solve it by finding the portion of a circumference. So, the radius is 10cm, which means the circumference is 2πr = 2π*10 = 20π cm. We can then take 60 degrees and divide it by the total 360 degrees in a circle. 60/360 = 1/6. you can then multiply (1/6) with the 20π circumference. (1/6)*20π = 20π/6, which simplifies to 10π/3

28. an arc of the circle measures 90° if the radius of the circle is 7cm what is the length of the arc​


ARC LENGTH

==================================

[tex]\large\sf\underline{Problem:} [/tex]

An arc of the circle measures 90° if the radius of the circle is 7cm what is the length of the arc?

==================================

[tex]\large\sf\underline{Answer:}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad \: \huge \tt 10.9 \: cm[/tex]

==================================

[tex]\large\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

The diameter of a circle is twice it's, therefore determine the arc length using the equation:

[tex]\bold{Formula\:||\:Arc_{Length} = \frac{n}{360°} \:\cdot\: 2πr}[/tex]

suppose the circle is 90° ,and the radius of a circle is 7 cm.Fill in the arc length equation. It will look like this:

[tex]\tt Arc_{Length} = \frac{90°}{360°} \:\cdot\: 2π(7\:cm) [/tex]

[tex]\tt Arc_{Length} = \frac{1}{4} \:\cdot\: π(14\:cm) [/tex]

[tex]\tt Arc_{Length} = \frac{(14\:cm)π }{4}[/tex]

[tex]\tt Arc_{Length} = π(3.5\:cm) [/tex]

Let 3.14 be the approximate value of pi.

[tex]\tt Arc_{Length} = (3.14)(3.5\:cm)[/tex]

[tex]\tt Arc_{Length} = 10.9 [/tex]

Therefore, the length of the arc is 10.9 cm
