14. Mary is four times as old as her daughter. Next year their ages will total 42 years. How old are they now​



Let's call Mary's age "M"
Let's call Mary's daughter's age "D"
From the problem, we know that:

M = 4D (since Mary is four times as old as her daughter)
M+1 + D+1 = 42 (since next year their ages will total 42 years)
We can use the first equation to substitute M in the second equation:

4D+1 + D+1 = 42
5D+2 = 42
5D = 40
D = 8
Now that we know the daughter's age, we can use the first equation to find Mary's age:

M = 4D
M = 4(8)
M = 32
Therefore, Mary is 32 years old and her daughter is 8 years old.

20. mark is twice as old as jenny.three years from now the sum of there ages will be 42.how old is mark?​


Answer:

Mark is 24 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Age Word Problem

Age word problems are problems dealing with the ages of people currently, in the past or in the future.

Let us analyze the problem.

Given:

Let x be Jenny's age.

Age Now:

"Mark is twice as old as Jenny."

Jenny - x

Mark - 2x

In Three Years:

Jenny - x + 3

Mark - 2x + 3

Equation:

"Three years from now, the sum of their ages will be 42."

x + 3 + 2x + 3 = 42

Solution:

Jenny's Age:

x + 3 + 2x + 3 = 42

3x + 6 = 42

3x = 42 - 6

3x = 36

3x/3 = 36/3

x = 12

Jenny is 12 years old.

Mark's Age:

2x = 2(12) = 24

Mark is 24 years old.

Checking:

To check, let us see if the sum of their ages in three years is 42.

Jenny - 12 + 3 = 15

Mark - 2(12) + 3 = 24 + 3 = 27

15 + 27 = 42 ✔
