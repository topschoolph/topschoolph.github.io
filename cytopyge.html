3. All are applications of exfoliative cytology


Answer:

Exfoliative cytology is a simple non-aggressive technique that is well accepted by the patient, and that is therefore an attractive option for the early diagnosis of oral cancer, including epithelial atypias and especially squamous cell carcinoma. However, traditional exfoliative cytology methods show low sensitivity (i.e. a high proportion of false negatives) in the diagnosis of these pathologies. This low sensitivity is attributable to various factors, including inadequate sampling, procedural errors, and the need for subjective interpretation of the findings. More recently, the continuing development of automated cytomorphometric methods, DNA content determination, tumour marker detection, and diverse molecular-level analyses has contributed to renewed interest in exfoliative cytology procedures for the diagnosis of oral cancer. 22. Biologist who contributed in the field of cytology


Cytology, or also known as cell biology, is a huge branch of biology that deals with the study of cells. There have been a number of biologists who contributed in the field of cytology, but let's focus on the pioneers. Below are the list of famous cytologists who contributed to the development of cytology.

Anton van Leewenhoek - the first person to observe microorganisms which he called animalscules. He was the first to invent and use a functional simple microscope to observe microorganisms.Robert Hooke - he developed his own version of microscope using two lenses. This produced a greater magnification enough to observe the basic unit of life. He observed that these looked like the cells of a monk in a monastery, hence he called these structures cells.Robery Brown - he discovered a solid sphere inside the cells of the roots of the orchid plants that he observed under the microscope. He called these structures nucleus.Matthias Schleiden and Theodore Schwann - they observed plant and animal cells. From their observation, they were able to arrive at a conclusion that the basis of life for all organisms are cells. This eventually lead to the cell theory of life.Rudolf Virchow - was the first person to explain that cells come from pre-existing cells and not from spontaneous generation. He wasn't able to fully establish this until the experiment of Louis Pasteur which have shown that cells only arise from pre-existing cells.
