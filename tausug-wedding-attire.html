3. who officiates the wedding of Tausug people​


Answer:

parents

Explanation:

Marriage is ideally arranged by parents. Contacts between the sexes are restricted and marriageable women are kept in relative seclusion to protect their value to their family as political and economic assets. First and second cousins are favored spouses (with the exception of the children of brothers). A series of negotiations precedes marriage, concluding with an agreement on the amount of bride-wealth and other expenses to be paid by the boy's family. In addition to arranged marriages, wives may be obtained by elopement or abduction, both common alternatives. Weddings are held in the groom's parents' house immediately upon payment of bride-wealth and are officiated by an imam.

22. differentiate the attires (color, pattern, and accessories) of the following ethnic groups with one another • Tausug • Yakan • Maranao​


Tausug- They use Muslim motifs called ukkil or okir, which are made up of geometric and flowing arabesque designs.

Male Tausugs wear badju lapi (jacket), sawwal (pants), kandit (sash), and pis siabit (sash) (square piece of cloth tied around the head).

Biyatawi (top), tambuku (gold buttons), and habul tiyahian (bottom) for females (a rectangular cloth draped across the shoulder).

Yakan- Yakans are known for their amazing technicolor geometric weaves and distinctive face decorations, which are used in traditional ceremonies.

Maranao- Tribal and Muslim influences can be seen in the designs and curvilinear motifs, such as stylized vines and the sarimanok.
