"BNHS Celebrates Prefixes and Suffixes"

On March 25, 2023, the Bagong Nayon National High School (BNHS) celebrated its 52nd Foundation Day. It was a day filled with various activities, including a parade, a program, and a series of contests that showcased the talents and skills of the students. While watching the festivities, I couldn't help but think about the importance of prefixes and suffixes in the English language.

Prefixes and suffixes are essential components of English words. They are added to the beginning or end of a root word to create a new word with a different meaning. Prefixes come before the root word, while suffixes come after the root word. For example, the word "unhappy" has the prefix "un-" and the root word "happy," while the word "happiness" has the suffix "-ness" added to the root word "happy."

Knowing prefixes and suffixes can help improve your vocabulary, comprehension, and communication skills. By understanding their meanings, you can decipher unfamiliar words and make more sense of the text you are reading or hearing. For example, if you come across the word "impossible," you can break it down to "im-" meaning "not" and "possible," which means "able to be done." So, "impossible" means "not able to be done."

During the BNHS Foundation Day program, I noticed how many of the students used words with prefixes and suffixes in their speeches and performances. They used words like "misunderstood," "disadvantageous," and "unforgettable" to express their thoughts and feelings. It was clear that they had a good grasp of these word components and how they contribute to the English language.

In conclusion, celebrating the BNHS Foundation Day reminded me of the importance of prefixes and suffixes in the English language. They are powerful tools that can help us communicate better, understand texts more easily, and express ourselves more effectively. By learning them, we can expand our vocabulary and become more proficient in English.

19. 40, 33, 26, 19, Determine if the sequence is arithmetic. If it is, find the common difference and the 52nd term.


Answer:

d = -7

52nd term = -317

Step-by-step explanation:

In arithmetic sequences there is what we call the common difference. That is, the difference between two numbers is always the same.

[tex]a_2-a_1 = a_3 - a_2 = a_4 - a_3 = ... = a_n - a_{n-1} = d\\33 - 40 = 26 - 33 = 19 - 26 = -7[/tex]

To find the nth term of an arithmetic sequence we use the following formula:

[tex]a_n = a_1 + (n-1)(d)[/tex]

Substitute what is given to get the 52nd term,

[tex]a_{52} = 40 + (52-1)(-7)\\a_{52} = 40 + (51)(-7)\\a_{52} = 40 - 357\\a_{52} = -317[/tex]

Therefore, the 52nd term is -317.

23. 2. Mharc Rolance saves as follows: 60 on the first week; 100 on the second week; ? 140 on the third week, and so on, for one year or a total of 52 weeks. Can you identify the amount of his 52nd deposit?with solutions


The 52nd deposit is 2100

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

a1 = 60

a2 = 100

a3 = 140

d = 40

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

52nd deposit

Step 3: Solve for the 52nd term of the sequence

Solution:

The formula for the nth term of an arithmetic series:

an = a1 +d (n - 1)

where: an - nth term, (a1, a2, a3, ... , an)

a1 - first term

d - common difference

n - number/order of terms

Substitute the value of a1, d and n

a52 = 60 + [40(52-1)]

a52 = 60 + [40(51)]

a52 = 2100
