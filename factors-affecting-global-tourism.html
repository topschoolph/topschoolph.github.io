12. Differentiate impact of world tourism on globalization from impact of globalization on world tourism.​


The impact of world tourism on globalization refers to the ways in which the growth of the global tourism industry has contributed to the process of globalization. Globalization is the process by which countries, economies, and societies become more interconnected and interdependent through the increasing exchange of goods, services, ideas, and cultural practices.

World tourism has played a significant role in the process of globalization by facilitating the movement of people across national borders and promoting the exchange of goods and services. The growth of the global tourism industry has led to the development of new transportation and communication technologies, such as low-cost airlines and online booking platforms, which have made it easier and more affordable for people to travel internationally. This has facilitated the growth of global tourism and increased the mobility of people, goods, and ideas.

At the same time, globalization has also had a significant impact on world tourism. The growth of global economic and political integration has led to an increase in the number of people with disposable income and the ability to travel internationally. This has created new opportunities for the global tourism industry, as more people are able to afford to travel and experience different cultures. Additionally, the growth of the global economy has led to the development of new destinations and attractions, such as theme parks, luxury resorts, and cultural festivals, which have increased the appeal of international travel.

Overall, the impact of world tourism on globalization and the impact of globalization on world tourism are intertwined and interconnected. The growth of the global tourism industry has facilitated the process of globalization, while globalization has also created new opportunities for the tourism industry. Both factors have contributed to the increasing interconnectedness of the world and the growth of international travel and exchange.

The growth and development of the tourism industry has a direct impact on the process of globalization. This can be seen in the economic effects of tourism, as well as the cultural and social impacts of increased travel and interaction between people from different countries. The ways in which globalization has affected the tourism industry include factors such as the growing ease of travel and communication, the expansion of international tourism markets, and the development of global tourism networks and destinations. The relationship between world tourism and globalization is complex and multifaceted, with the growth of the tourism industry both driving and being driven by globalization. Globalization has had both positive and negative impacts on the tourism industry and on the countries and communities that host tourists.

15. Explain how global tourism organizations affect the government policy of a country as regard to tourism industry and its sectors.​


Answer:

Abstract

Darwin, the capital city of the Northern Territory, has recently received high levels of government investment in tourism development. This paper examines the role of the Northern Territory Government in the development and marketing of tourism in Darwin and investigates the types of strategies that the government has sought to implement over the past decade. The results suggest that the Northern Territory Government has been using tourism primarily as a vehicle to boost immediate short-term economic and employment growth by investing in large-scale “showy” construction projects. Government sponsored initiatives in tourism are highly growth-orientated and show a tendency towards “superlativism”. The paper finally discusses a range of limitations and implications of such strong government patronage of tourism and identifies directions for future research.
