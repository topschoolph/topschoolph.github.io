Standard conditions for temperature and pressure are used as a reference in certain calculations or conditions

There are several standards used by various organizations such as IUPAC or NIST

There are 2 conditions that are usually used as a reference in chemical calculations (mainly for determining the volume per mole of a gas or the molar volume), namely:

1. Standard Conditions

Conditions at T 0 ° C (273.15 K) and P 1 atm are stated by STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure). At STP, Vm is 22.4 liters / mol.

2 Room Condition

Conditions at T 25 ° C (298 K) and P 1 atm are stated by RTP (Room Temperature and Pressure). Vm in this condition = 24.4 liters / mol

19. 10.0 L of nitrogen gas is produced at 23oC and 101 kPa. What must the new temperature be if the volume is increased to 12.0 L at 115 kPa?


Answer:

t2 = 131 °C

Explanation:

Convert °C to K

T1 = 23 °C + 273 = 296 K

Use Combined Gas Law to solve for the final pressure temperature

P2V2 / T2 = P1V1 / T1

T2 = T1 • (V2 / V1) • (P2 / P1)

T2 = 296 K • (12.0 L / 10.0L) • (115 kPa / 101 kPa)

Convert K to °C

t2 = 404 K - 273

23. A sample of a gas has a volume of 20 L at 1.5 atm. What will be the volume of this gas at each of the followed pressure: a.) 700 mmHg b.) 2 atm c.) 105 kpa


To solve this problem, we can use the combined gas law equation, which relates the pressure, volume, and temperature of a gas:

(P1 × V1) / T1 = (P2 × V2) / T2

where P1, V1, and T1 are the initial pressure, volume, and temperature, and P2, V2, and T2 are the new pressure, volume, and temperature.

We can assume that the temperature remains constant, so we can simplify the equation to:

P1 × V1 = P2 × V2

To find the new volume of the gas at a different pressure, we can rearrange the equation as follows:

V2 = (P1 × V1) / P2

a.) To find the volume of the gas at 700 mmHg, we need to convert 1.5 atm to mmHg and substitute the values into the equation:

V2 = (1.5 atm × 760 mmHg/atm × 20 L) / 700 mmHg
V2 ≈ 65 L

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 700 mmHg is approximately 65 L.

b.) To find the volume of the gas at 2 atm, we can substitute the values into the equation:

V2 = (1.5 atm × 20 L) / 2 atm
V2 = 15 L

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 2 atm is 15 L.

c.) To find the volume of the gas at 105 kPa, we need to convert 1.5 atm to kPa and substitute the values into the equation:

V2 = (1.5 atm × 101.325 kPa/atm × 20 L) / 105 kPa
V2 ≈ 29 L

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 105 kPa is approximately 29 L.

30. A sample of a gas has a volume of 20 L at 1.5 atm. What will be the volume of this gas at each of the followed pressure: a.) 700 mmHg b.) 2 atm c.) 105 kpa


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the combined gas law, which relates the initial and final conditions of pressure, volume, and temperature for a fixed amount of gas:

(P1 x V1)/T1 = (P2 x V2)/T2

where P1, V1, and T1 are the initial pressure, volume, and temperature, respectively, and P2, V2, and T2 are the final pressure, volume, and temperature, respectively.

Since the amount of gas is fixed, we can assume that the temperature remains constant, so we can simplify the equation to:

P1 x V1 = P2 x V2

Now we can use this equation to solve for the final volume at each of the given pressures:

a.) To convert 1.5 atm to mmHg, we can use the conversion factor: 1 atm = 760 mmHg

1.5 atm x (760 mmHg/1 atm) = 1140 mmHg

So the equation becomes:

(1140 mmHg x 20 L) = (700 mmHg x V2)

V2 = (1140 mmHg x 20 L)/700 mmHg = 32.57 L

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 700 mmHg is 32.57 L.

b.) The equation becomes:

(1.5 atm x 20 L) = (2 atm x V2)

V2 = (1.5 atm x 20 L)/2 atm = 15 L

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 2 atm is 15 L.

c.) To convert 1.5 atm to kPa, we can use the conversion factor: 1 atm = 101.325 kPa

1.5 atm x (101.325 kPa/1 atm) = 151.988 kPa

So the equation becomes:

(151.988 kPa x 20 L) = (105 kPa x V2)

V2 = (151.988 kPa x 20 L)/105 kPa = 29.05 L

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 105 kPa is 29.05 L.
