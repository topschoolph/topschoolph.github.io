9. What is the MASS in grams of a lead (Pb) brick that measures 20.5 cm, 9.7 cm, 62mm? The density of lead is 11.4 g/cm³


Answer:

14,054.718 grams

Explanation:

The density is given to be [tex]\frac{mass}{volume}[/tex], and to get the mass in grams, we need to calculate for the volume first.

Calculating the volume of the brick,  we get:

[tex]V = L * W * H = 20.5 cm * 9.7 cm * 62 mm * (\frac{1 cm}{10 mm}) = 1232.87 cm^{3}[/tex]

After, multiply the volume of the brick to the density to get the mass in grams.

[tex]m = density * volume = 11.4 \frac{g}{cm^{3} } * 1232.87 cm^{3} = 14,054.718 grams[/tex]

The volume will cancel out, leaving us with the mass.

density = weight / volume

volume = length * width * height

volume = 230.75 cm^3

density = weight / volume

density = 9.95 g / 230.75 cm^3

density = 0.0432 g/cm^3

density = weight / volume

Density = 500g / 2500cm3

Density = mass/volume

Density = 6.0g / 12.0cm3

3. The density of a substance is 4.0 g/cm3. If a sample of the substance has a volume of 25 cm3, then what is its mass? (Hint: Use the equation: mass = density × volume.)

Mass = density × volume

Mass = 4.0 g/cm3 × 25 cm3

Mass = 100 g

Volume = mass/density

Volume = 420 g / 10.5 g/cm3

Volume = 40 cm3
