12. WORD JUMBLE . Direction: Rearrange the jumbled letters to form the correct word or words related to cellular respiration. Hint is provided for each item. 1. LGOYSLYCS (1 word) 2. KRCBSCLEYE (2 words) 3. EELCOTRNTARNOSPRTCHINA (3 words) 4. GULSCOE (1 word) 5. ANDEOISNETRIHPOSHTPAE (2 words)​ NEED HELP


Answer:

1. LGOYSLYCS - Glycolysis

2. KRCBSCLEYE - Krebs Cycle

3.EELCOTRNTARNOSPRTCHINA - Electron Transport Chain

4. GULSCOE - Glucose

5. ANDEOISNETRIHPOSHTPAE - Adenosine Triphosphate

Explanation:

Glycolysis

The first step in getting energy out of food. Glycolysis breaks one glucose molecule down into two molecules of pyruvate. Glycolysis happens in almost all organisms, and it can take place with or without oxygen. Here at Shmoop, we are pretty sure the U2 song "With or Without You" was really about glycolysis and oxygen.

Krebs Cycle

A synonym for the citric acid cycle. Why do biologists give one process multiple names? We'll never know. The name Krebs cycle pays homage to the man who discovered it, Hans Krebs.

Electron Transport Chain

The step-by-step process of removing electrons from NADH and FADH2 to power ATP synthesis.

Adenosine Triphosphate

The end-all, be-all to a cell. Short for adenosine triphosphate, ATP is energy to a cell, and without energy, life is a no go. ATP is the main attraction in cellular respiration.

Step-by-step explanation:

The word legit is a shorthand term for the word legitimate. This word means something that is legal, lawful, or done in compliance with the set rules and regulations.

Synonyms are words that share the same meaning. Since these words have the same definition, they can be used interchangeably in a sentence or in a conversation.

Antonyms on the other hand, are the antithesis of synonyms. These words do not share the same meaning and are even opposite from each other. Antonyms are usually used to contrast characteristics from each other.
