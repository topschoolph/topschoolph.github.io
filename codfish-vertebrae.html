7. What is the job of the vertebrae?


Answer:

The vertebrae are a series of bones that make up the spinal column. They play a vital role in protecting the spinal cord and supporting the weight of the upper body. Here are some of the jobs of the vertebrae:

•Protect the spinal cord: The spinal cord is a bundle of nerves that runs down the back of the body. The vertebrae surround and protect the spinal cord, providing a shield against injury.

•Support the upper body: The vertebrae help support the weight of the upper body, including the head, neck, and torso. The spinal column also provides attachment points for various muscles and ligaments, which helps to stabilize the upper body.

•Allow for movement: The vertebrae are separated by intervertebral discs, which act as shock absorbers and allow for movement between the bones. This movement enables the body to bend, twist, and move in various ways.

•Transmit nerve signals: The vertebrae also serve as channels through which nerve signals can travel from the brain to the rest of the body. The nerves that pass through the spinal cord branch out to various parts of the body, allowing for movement and sensation.

In summary, the vertebrae serve several essential functions in the body. They protect the spinal cord, support the upper body, allow for movement, and transmit nerve signals. These jobs are critical to maintaining good overall health and mobility.

20. vertebrae in the spinal column of a child​


Answer:

The vertebral column usually consists of 33 vertebrae: 24 presacral vertebrae (7 cervical, 12 thoracic, and 5 lumbar) followed by the sacrum (5 fused sacral vertebrae) and the coccyx (4 frequently fused coccygeal vertebrae). The 24 presacral vertebrae allow movement and hence render the vertebral column flexible.
