Mouth Stomach Small Intestine Esophagus Large IntestineD. Mouth Esophagus --- Stomach -- Small intestine Large Intestine​


DIGESTIVE SYSTEM

What are the correct pathways of food in digestive system?

ANSWER

Amongst the given choices above, the correct answer is letter D. Mouth -- Esophagus --- Stomach -- Small intestine Large Intestine.

WHY?

Of course the food will be swallowed first through the mouth, then to the throat (larynx), into the esophagus, to the stomach, then the food's nutrients and vitamins will be absorbed in small intestine, and finally the waste food materials will be processed in the large intestine to become waste products.

25. Direction: Write the uses of the following tools and materials1.Hammer2. Screwdriver3. Combination Pliers4. Switch5. Wire stripper6. Stubby screw driver7. Circuit Breaker8. Allen wrench9. Clamp10. Long nose pliers​


Answer:

HAMMER

a tool with a heavy metal head mounted at right angles at the end of a handle, used for jobs such as breaking things and driving in nails.

SCREWDRIVER

a tool with a flattened, cross-shaped, or star-shaped tip that fits into the head of a screw to turn it.

COMBINATION PLIERS

slip-joint pliers with a notched inner grip for holding and grasping round objects and cutting and bending wire

SWITCH

a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit.

WIRE STRIPPER

A wire stripper is a small, hand-held device used to strip the electrical insulation from electric wires.

STUBBY SCREW DRIVER

A stubby screwdriver is a tool used to facilitate access to screws located in areas that cannot be reached easily by a regular screwdriver

CIRCUIT BREAKER

A circuit breaker is an automatically operated electrical switch designed to protect an electrical circuit from damage caused by excess current from an overload or short circuit.

ALLEN WRENCH

an L-shaped metal bar with a hexagonal head at each end, used to turn bolts and screws having hexagonal sockets.

CLAMP

Clamps are versatile tools that serve to temporarily hold work securely in place. They are used for many applications including carpentry, woodworking, furniture making, welding, construction and metal working.

LONG NOSE PLIERS

Use to grip small objects, reach awkward places, holding wires, bend loops, and attach wires. Work involving smaller gauge wire.
