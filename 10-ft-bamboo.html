1. A 10 ft. bamboo ladder is placed against the wall with its base 3 ft. from the wall. find the vertical distance of the top of the bamboo ladder to the ground.​


[tex]\bold {PROBLEM:}[/tex]

A 10 ft. bamboo ladder is placed against the wall with its base 3 ft. from the wall. find the vertical distance of the top of the bamboo ladder to the ground.

[tex]\bold {SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Using the pythagorean theorem,

[tex] \large \tt {c}^{2} = {a}^{2} + {b}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt {10}^{2} = {3}^{2} + {b}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt {10}^{2} - {3}^{2} = {b}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt 100 - 9 = {b}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt 91 = {b}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt \sqrt{91} = \sqrt{ {b}^{2} } \\ \\ \green{\boxed{ \begin{array}{l}\large \tt b = \sqrt{91} \: ft. \\ \\ \large \tt b \approx9.54 \: ft.\end{array}}}[/tex]

∴ The vertical distance of the top of the bamboo ladder to the ground is approximately 9.54 ft.

29. 4. B. Which of the following will be the length of the diagonal of a square whose side measures 10 ft. long? A. 5 ft. 50 C. 10√2 ft. D. 20 ft.​


The diagonal of a square can be found using the Pythagorean theorem, which states that the square of the length of the diagonal is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the two sides of the square.

In this case, the length of one side of the square is given as 10 feet. Therefore, the length of the diagonal can be found as:

diagonal^2 = 10^2 + 10^2
diagonal^2 = 200
diagonal = sqrt(200)
diagonal = 10sqrt(2)

So, the length of the diagonal of the square is 10√2 feet.

Therefore, the answer is C. 10√2 ft.
