9. what are the disavantages of using non standard unit of measuring in getting the longth,speed on distance traveled by on object?​


Answer:

Lack of standardization: Non-standard units are not consistent or uniform, and can vary from person to person, region to region, or even from one measurement to another. This can lead to confusion, errors, and inaccuracies.

Difficulty in communication: Non-standard units may not be widely recognized or understood, making it difficult to communicate measurements accurately or effectively to others.

Limited application: Non-standard units are usually limited in their use and may not be suitable for certain applications or fields. For example, using hand spans to measure distance would not be suitable for measuring the length of a room or the distance between two cities.

Incompatibility with standard units: Non-standard units may not be compatible with standard units of measurement, making it difficult to compare or convert measurements between different systems.

Difficulty in calculation: Non-standard units may not have convenient conversion factors, making calculations more complex and time-consuming.

15. Give the mathematical equation of Speed of Sound at standard temperature. identify each details - units.​


Explanation:

The faster a sound wave travels, the more distance it will cover in the same period of time. If a sound wave were observed to travel a distance of 700 meters in 2 seconds, then the speed of the wave would be 350 m/s. A slower wave would cover less distance - perhaps 660 meters - in the same time period of 2 seconds and thus have a speed of 330 m/s. Faster waves cover more distance in the same period of time.

•The faster an object is moving, the longer the distance it takes to stop. If a vehicle's speed doubles, it needs about 4X's the distance to stop. If a vehicle's speed triples, it needs up to 9X's the distance to stop. Weight works in the same way that speed does in relation to stopping distances.

How can you tell if something is in motion?

•An object is in motion if its distance relative to another object is changing. To tell if an object is moving, you use a reference point. If an object's distance from another object [reference point] is changing. A place or object used for comparison to determine if something is in motion.
