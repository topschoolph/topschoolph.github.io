Research design definition

Research design is the framework of research methods and techniques chosen by a researcher. The design allows researchers to hone in on research methods that are suitable for the subject matter and set up their studies up for success.

The design of a research topic explains the type of research (experimental, survey research, correlational, semi-experimental, review) and also its sub-type (experimental design, research problem, descriptive case-study).  

There are three main types of designs for research: Data collection, measurement, and analysis.

The type of research problem an organization is facing will determine the research design and not vice-versa. The design phase of a study determines which tools to use and how they are used.

9. 1. What makes a research design quantitative? 2. Do you think the methodology in the research design is specific and attainable? 3. What is the step-by-step procedure the researcher should take in order to implement the research design effectively?


Answer:

1. A research design is quantitative when it involves the collection and analysis of numerical data to answer a research question. This data can be collected through surveys, experiments, or other forms of data collection.

2. Yes, the methodology in the research design should be specific and attainable. The researcher should have a clear understanding of the research question, the data sources, and the methods they will use to collect and analyze the data. They should also have an idea of the timeline for the research project, including when data will be collected and when results will be reported.

3. The step-by-step procedure for implementing the research design effectively will depend on the specific research project. Generally, the researcher should begin by developing a research plan, which should include a clear research question, a timeline, a data collection plan, and a data analysis plan. Once the plan is in place, the researcher should begin collecting the data, analyze the data, and draw conclusions based on the results. The researcher should also document the entire process, including any changes made to the plan, data collection, and analysis. Finally, the researcher should present their findings in a report or other format.
