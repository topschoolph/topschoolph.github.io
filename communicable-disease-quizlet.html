PsoriasisThis is a disease that is caused due to the speedy mass production  of skin in a person's body.A person infected by this disease will notice symptoms of having red and scaly patches in their skin.This is mainly caused by genetics, and cannot be spread.

20. write C if ts is communicable disease and NC if not communicable disease


COMMUNICABLE AND NON-COMMUNICABLE DISEASESDEFINITIONSCOMMUNICABLE DISEASES

- These are diseases that can be transferred from one human to another through a carrier.

- This carrier may be bodily fluids (e.g. blood, sweat, semen, etc.), insect bites, inhaling the particular virus itself, and more.

NON-COMMUNICABLE DISEASES

- These are diseases that cannot be transferred from one person to another.

- They are most likely not a result of foreign invasion, and are most likely a result of internal malfunction.

COMMUNICABLE VS NON-COMMUNICABLEFilariasis

- This is a communicable disease.

- It is a parasitic disease caused by the spread of thread-like worms that are microscopic.

- The disease spreads through mosquito bites, wherein a mosquito infected with Filariasis by a sick individual's blood, may be able to transfer it to another person.

Malaria

- This is a communicable disease.

- This is a fatal disease that attacks the red blood cells, one of the most important substituent components of the circulatory system.

- This can be spread through mosquito bites, wherein a mosquito carrying the parasite will transfer it to a human host whilst they feed on their blood, and vice versa.

Ringworm

- This is a communicable disease.

- It is an infectious disease that develops when fungal spores reproduce in an outer layer of dead skin.

- This can spread by simply touching an infected person or using their materials, especially those used in the bathroom.

Psoriasis

- This is a non-communicable disease.

- It is a genetic disease wherein red, scaly patches appear on the skin.

Bird's Flu

- This is a communicable disease.

- Bird Flu, or more commonly known as Avian Flu, is a virus that has the ability to infect not only birds, but also humans and other animals as well.

- Avian Flu is a similarly worse version of the regular influenza, having the potential to cause death in people who did not have any prior immune system-weakening events prior to the flu.

HIV

- This is a communicable disease.

- It is a virus that attacks the immune system, crippling it, and in the long term, destroying it completely.

- It can be spread through certain bodily fluids (e.g. semen, blood, vaginal fluids, etc.)

27. characteristic of communicable disease and non communicable disease​


Answer:

A communicable disease is one that is spread from one person to another through a variety of ways that include: contact with blood and bodily fluids; breathing in an airborne virus; or by being bitten by an insect.

A non-communicable disease (NCD) is a disease that is not transmissible directly from one person to another. NCDs include Parkinson's disease, autoimmune diseases, strokes, most heart diseases, most cancers, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, osteoarthritis, osteoporosis, Alzheimer's disease, cataracts, and others.
