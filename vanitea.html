6. 1/2 of the children at a party like chocolate ice cream, 2/3 like vanilla ice cream, and 1/3 like chocolate and vanilla. What fraction of those who like vanilla also like chocolate?


Answer:

[tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

First we need to take the total number of children who like vanilla which is 2/3  then we take the total number of children who like chocolate which is 1/2. Then we have the total of 1/3 who like vanilla and chocolate together.

Now that we have both values, we can then proceed to divide both of the children who like chocolate and who like chocolate and vanilla by each other.

This then gives us:

[tex]\frac{ \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{2}{3} }[/tex]

In fraction, we know that dividing a fraction with another fraction can also be the same as multiplying them together by getting the reciprocal of the second fraction.

We then have:

[tex]\frac{1}{3}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{3}{2}[/tex]

After cancelling out 3 from numerator and denominator of our fraction, we will get:

[tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

21. A vanilla cupcake costs twice as much as mocha cupcake. A vanilla and a mocha cupcake cost Php 73.50. How much is 3 mocha and 5 vanilla cupcake​


Vanilla cupcake= 2x
Mocha cupcake = x
Vanilla & mocha cupcake = Php73.50

To get the price of each cupcake, this will be the equation;
2x+x= 73.50

Solution
2x+x= 73.50
3x= 73.50

You need to divide 73.50 by 3 to get the price of the mocha cupcake .
73.50/3
= php24.50

The price of the mocha cupcake is Php24.50 so the vanilla cupcake will be Php49 because the price of it is twice the price of the mocha.

The question is how much is 3 mocha and 5 vanilla, so this will be the equation;

(3*Php24.50)+(5*Php49)
(‘*’ represents multiply)

Solution
3*24.50= 73.50
5*49=245
Php73.50 + Php245
= Php318.50

Answer
Php 318.50
