15. What musical instrument produces sounds by striking two mallets?


INSTRUMENTS PRODUCES SOUNDS BY STRIKING TWO MALLETS

Answer:

Membranophone

Explanation:

A membranophone is a type of musical instrument that produces sound by vibrating a stretched membrane. The mirliton, or kazoo, and the friction drum are two primary forms of drums (sounded by friction produced by drawing a stick back and forth through a hole in the membrane). Membranophones are the most prevalent types of drums. When the membrane or head of a membranophone is struck with a hand, mallet, stick, beater, or other improvised tool, sound is produced. Membranophones are musical instruments in which the vibrations of stretched skins or membranes produce sound. Membranophones include things like drums, tambourines, and even gongs. You may feel the membrane shake if you place your finger over the top of it. These vibrations cause sound to be produced.
