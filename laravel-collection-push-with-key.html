3. Activity 3: Pushing One Key The keys on a calculator look like this. You are to push one key without looking. What is the probability of pushing?​


Fundamental Concept

Probability is the likelihood of achieving the desired outcome.

This is calculated by dividing the number of desired outcomes by the total number of possible outcomes.

The higher the probability, the higher the likelihood of the event occurring.

Illustration

Say you have a jar with 1 red, 1 green, and 1 blue marble. The probability of getting a red marble is 1/3 since there is one red marble (the desired outcome) and there are 3 possible outcomes (being the red, green, and blue marble).

ApplicationThere are 3 numbers divisible by 3 (3, 6, 9) (desired outcome). There are a total of 16 keys in the calculator (total possible outcomes).

So the probability of pushing a number divisible by 3 is 3/16.
There are 3 numbers greater than 6 (7, 8, 9) (desired outcome). There are a total of 16 keys in the calculator (total possible outcomes).

So the probability of pushing a number greater than 6 is 3/16.
There are 4 operation signs ( +, -, x, ÷) (desired outcome). There are a total of 16 keys in the calculator (total possible outcomes).

So the probability of pushing an operation sign is 4/16 or simply 1/4.
Composite numbers are number with more than 2 factors. There are 4 composite numbers (4, 6, 8, 9) (desired outcome). There are a total of 16 keys in the calculator (total possible outcomes).

So the probability of pushing a composite number is 4/16 or simply 1/4.
There are 9 counting numbers (desired outcome). There are a total of 16 keys in the calculator (total possible outcomes).

So the probability of pushing a counting number is 9/16.
