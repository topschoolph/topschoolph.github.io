16. describe the unique lines ,color and shape in pakistan truck art


The indigenous art of a Pakistan truck.

The unique truck art in Pakistan is more than what our eyes can see. Every single stroke and color painted on a truck is deeply rooted to their culture, history and tradition. One must, at least, get to know their history to be able to fully understand the significance of every symbol decorated on a truck. Drivers would naturally spend more on decorating their vehicles than their own houses because this is seen as a business investment in their country. Clients would naturally hire a truck that is well decorated over one that is plain.

Some of the noted adornments you could see in a Pakistani truck art patterns are:

flowers - flora and fauna landscapes such as mountains and lakes or buildings portrait of some important religious saints or people calligraphy of their own local language different ornaments such as camel bones which is commonly seen in the region of Sindh.

The Pakistani truck art drawing could be described as very dynamic and flamboyant. Primary colors namely red, yellow and blue, as well as some secondary colors such as yellow and orange are the hues you would often see in almost all of the trucks. The interior could sometimes hold outlines of gold to highlight the patterns being presented. All of the illustrations are painted using actual lines which means they meant to be seen as two-dimentional drawings, laid flat and as visible and clear as it is in the naked eye. If one would look closely, it can be observed that patterns also vary by sections. For example, horizontal row of flowers could be seen on the window of the truck while a portrait could be seen on the door. This creates balance and a kaleidoscopic effect to anyone who looks at the vehicle. It is also good to note the calligraphy they paint on their vehicles. Certain texts are about religious expressions, poetry or an important cultural sayings in their country.  

Overall, this is a moving canvas that tells a story by giving you a glimpse of their very long tradition and culture. Please check out the links below if you wish to research more on Asian artworks and Pakistan's history.

30. how does religion affect the music of countries like india and pakistan?​


Answer:

Sound in its infinite varieties is of crucial importance to Indian religious thought. The universe itself is constituted of and by sound, and the omnipresence of sound envelops daily life. Hearing and listening to sound, therefore, are required of the individual to negotiate a path through life. Accordingly, the ontology of music depends on the physical perception of sound as a means of contemplation and worship. In Hinduism the primacy of hearing and listening for devotion is clearly evident in the term given to the foundational sacred scriptures, śruti (literally, "something that is heard"), which also refers to the fine divisions of pitch in Indian melodic modes, or rāgas. The aural perception of music also serves as the central ontology of music in Islam, in which the term samaʿ (both "hearing" and "listening") refers to music making and establishes the importance of aural perceptions, rather than sound production
