1. confining stress meaning​


Answer:

Is the force per unit area that is placed on a rock. A deeply buried rock is pushed down by the weight of all the material above it. Since the rock cannot move, it cannot deform. This is called confining stress.


2. example of confining stress?


Answer:

The weight of all the stuff atop a deeply buried rock pushes it down. The rock can't deform since it can't move. Confining stress is the term for this. Compression causes rocks to bend or fracture as they are squeezed together.
