4. what was the message that danton remoto to convey in this poem


Answer:

Here, read it.

Explanation:

From the first line, which says: “You are a continent away”, it is clear that the addressee is someone who, literally, is far away from the persona or in another way round, it can be an hyperbole thing because there’s a reason that it is just being exaggerated.. The author proceeds to use images relating to the seasons: “There, the leaves are beginning / to turn / Soon night will steal hours / from day / and snow will be whirling / in drifts.” Through the imagery, it is obvious that where the addressee is, the beginning of the season of autumn is taking place, with the transition into the season of winter slowly coming, and through that, the poem is able to further emphasize the distance separating the persona and the addressee. It is possible to think that based on these images, the addressee could be staying in a continent such as North America, where the seasons of autumn and winter do take place, and the persona in a continent such as Asia. The line “There the leaves are beginning to turn, soon night will steal hours from day/ and now still be whirling” also shows personification aspect as the night was given a human characteristic even if it is not a living thing and it was just a phenomenon.

What was the message that Danton Remoto wanted to convey in this poem?

The message that Danton Remoto wants to convey in this poem is to see Jose Rizal put to death, who wants to express the feelings of a teacher who is admiring the bravery of his beloved pupil. For a teacher, seeing how his student has changed at this moment is heartbreaking. The poet challenges readers to stand up for what is just. When the poet heard that Rizal would be put to death, she had goosebumps. Rizal, on the other hand, has no fear of passing away. She had just gone through a difficult ordeal, and her cheeks were still crimson. Through his writings, he persuaded the Filipinos to rebel against the Spanish empire, a decision he never looked back on. Despite not actively taking part in the uprising, he was crucial in guiding the populace. Therefore, the poet urges readers to remember Rizal's sacrifice and to stand up resolutely for what is right.
