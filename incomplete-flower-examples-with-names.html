29. parts and function of both complete and incomplete flowers. 5 examples PLSSSSS HELP MEH​


Answer:

Complete flowers have both male and female reproductive organs, while incomplete flowers have only one or the other.

The parts of a complete flower include:

Sepals: the green leaf-like structures that protect the bud before it blooms.Petals: the colorful, often fragrant structures that attract pollinators.Stamens: the male reproductive organs that produce pollen.Pistil: the female reproductive organ that receives pollen and produces seeds.

Examples of plants with complete flowers include:

RoseSunflowerPeonyAppleLilac

The parts of an incomplete flower include:

Sepals: the green leaf-like structures that protect the bud before it blooms.Petals: the colorful, often fragrant structures that attract pollinators.Stamens OR Pistil: the reproductive organs that are present, but not both.

Examples of plants with incomplete flowers include:

CornCucumberSquashPumpkinCactus

It's worth noting that, some plants have both complete and incomplete flowers on the same plant, these are called perfect flowers. They have both male and female reproductive organs, and they can self-fertilize.
