2. what are the three considered building blocks of conceptual knowledge?​


Concepts: These are mental categories that are used to group objects, events, or ideas that share similar characteristics. Examples include concepts like "car," "dog," or "friendship."

Propositions: These are statements that express relationships between concepts. They can be either true or false, and they help to build more complex understandings of the world. Examples include "Dogs are animals" or "John is friends with Mary."

Schemas: These are mental frameworks that help to organize and interpret information. They are often based on past experiences and can be used to make predictions or guide behavior. Examples include schemas for how to behave in a restaurant, or how to navigate a new city.
