8. is a driver allowed to drive on the transition lines?


Answer:

It is generally not allowed for a driver to drive on the transition lines that separate the lanes of traffic on a road. Transition lines, also known as lane markings, are used to indicate the separation between lanes of traffic and to guide drivers as they change lanes or make turns.

It is important for drivers to stay within their designated lanes and to avoid driving on or across the transition lines, as this can cause confusion and increase the risk of a collision. In some cases, driving on or across the transition lines may also be considered a traffic violation, which can result in fines and points on your driver's license.

To avoid driving on the transition lines, it is important to pay attention to the road and to maintain a safe distance from other vehicles. It is also a good idea to use your turn signals when changing lanes or making turns, to alert other drivers of your intentions.

The only difference is that the first driver’s car could drive 4 hours in that amount of petrol and the second one could drive 5 hours. However, they only drove for some time and found that the amount of petrol that was left in one of the cars was four times the petrol left in the other one. For how long had they driven at this point in time? I will report nonsense answers

3.75 hours

Explanation:

While you can solve it as you like, a simple mathematical equation can be used to find out.

Let M be the amount of petrol initially.

Let N be the time for which they drove.

According to the question, the amount of petrol used by first car in N hours = MN / 4

The amount of petrol used by second car in N hours = MN / 5

Hence, the amount of petrol left in the first car = (M - MN / 4)

The amount of petrol left in the second car = (M - MN / 5)

AS per the details given in the question, we can form the below equation:

M - MN / 5 = 4(M - MN / 4)

N = 15 / 4 or 3.75 hours.

Hence, both the drivers have driven the car for 3.75 hours at that particular time.
