2. There are 4 less members in Team Red than Team Blue. If 7 members are transferred from Team Red to Team Blue, the total number of members in Team Red becomes 1/3 the number of Team Blue. How many members are there in Team Red and Team Blue respectively?​


Answer:

Let x be the number of members in Team Blue.

Since there are 4 less members in Team Red than Team Blue, Team Red has x - 4 members.

After 7 members are transferred from Team Red to Team Blue, the total number of members in Team Red becomes 1/3 the number of Team Blue.

So, (x - 4 + 7) = 1/3(x)

So, x - 4 + 7 = x/3

We can now simplify this equation by multiplying both sides by 3

3x - 12 + 21 = x

2x = 33

x = 16

So Team Blue has 16 members

Team Red has x - 4 = 16 - 4 = 12 members.

Therefore, Team Red has 12 members and Team Blue has 16 members


3. 3. Red cards numbered 1 to 6 and blue cards numbered 7 to 10. What is the probability of getting a blue card or an even number? Formula used is non-mutually exclusive events. (Please show solution on how to do it.) Thank you!


There are 10 cards in total, with 4 of them being even numbers and 4 of them being blue cards. One of the blue cards is also an even number.

Let A = event of drawing a blue card

Let B = event of drawing an even number

P(A) = 4/10 = 2/5

P(B) = 4/10 = 2/5

However, we must subtract the probability of drawing the one card that satisfies both conditions (i.e. the blue even card) to avoid counting it twice:

P(A and B) = 1/10

Using the formula for non-mutually exclusive events:

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)

= (2/5) + (2/5) - (1/10)

= 4/10

= 2/5

Therefore, the probability of drawing a blue card or an even number is 2/5.

27. A cube has 6 faces that are red, yellow, or blue. Maggie rolls the cube 20 times and records the color facing up. Color Number of Occurrences Red 7 Yellow 6 Blue 7 Based on the data, which is the BEST conclusion about the colors of the cube? The cube has 4 red faces, 1 blue face, and 1 yellow face. The cube has 2 red faces, 3 blue faces, and 1 yellow face. The cube has 2 red faces, 2 blue faces, and 2 yellow faces. The cube has 1 red face, 4 blue faces, and 1 yellow face.


Answer:

The cube has 2 red faces, 2 blue faces, and 2 yellow faces.

Step-by-step explanation:

Solution 1:

It would be possible to take your time and solve for the probabilities of each of the given choices and compare it to the given, but it would really save your time to analyze the given first.

Notice, that the number of occurrences for red, yellow, and blue are more or less equal. Hence, we can say that the chances of the cube landing on each color is the same. This means that each color has 6 / 3 = 2 faces.

Therefore, the cube has 2 red faces, 2 blue faces, and 2 yellow faces.

Solution 2:

This is the longer option if you'd really opt to see the numbers and computations. Let us make a table of the probability per color for each of the situations including the given.

Answers and Explanations

1. B: On a six-sided die, the probability of throwing any number is 1 in 6. The probability of throwing a 3 or a 4 is double that, or 2 in 6. This can be simplified by dividing both 2 and 6 by 2.

Therefore, the probability of throwing either a 3 or 4 is 1 in 3.

2. D: Shown below is the sample space of possible outcomes for tossing three coins, one at a time. Since there is a possibility of two outcomes (heads or tails) for each coin, there is a total of 2*2*2=8 possible outcomes for the three coins altogether. Note that H represents heads and T represents tails:

HHH HHT HTT HTH TTT TTH THT THH

Notice that out of the 8 possible outcomes, only 3 of them (HHT, HTH, and THH) meet the desired condition that two coins land heads up and one coin lands tails up. Probability, by definition, is the number of desired outcomes divided by the number of possible outcomes. Therefore, the probability of two heads and one tail is 3/8, Choice D.

3. E: There are 90 two-digit numbers (all integers from 10 to 99). Of those, there are 13 multiples of 7: 14, 21, 28, 35, 42, 49, 56, 63, 70, 77, 84, 91, 98.

5. D: Use this ratio for probability:

Probability = Number of Desired Outcomes

Number of Possible Outcomes

There are 6 blue marbles and 3 red marbles for a total of 9 desired outcomes. Add the total number of marbles to get the total number of possible outcomes, 14. The probability that a red or blue marble will be selected is 9/14.
