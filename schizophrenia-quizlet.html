19. what is the treatment of schizophrenia​


Explanation:

Schizophrenia requires lifelong treatment, even if the symptoms subside. Treatment with medications and psychosocial therapy can help manage the condition. In some cases, hospitalization may be required. A psychiatrist experienced in the treatment of schizophrenia usually guides the treatment.

23. misconceptions about schizophrenia


There are a lot of misconceptions of schizophrenia. This is partly because of lack of education about the mental disorder and eventually people having wrong judgments about it. This results to a great social stigma about schizophrenia patients and the people around them. Listed below are some of the common misconceptions about schizophrenia.

Schizophrenic patients have multiple personalities. They don't. That is what we call MPD or multiple personality disorder. It is a totally different mental condition.Schizophrenia make people dangerous. While it is undeniable that part of the symptoms is being violent, not all patients are violent. Some are docile but cannot focus well.Schizophrenia's only symptoms are delusions and hallucinations. The disease also involves memory loss up to some extent or a great deal of disorientation.Schizophrenia is nontreatable. It can be treated with enough support from people around the patient and continued medication.

The exact cause of schizophrenia is still on hot debate but the studies have shown that it is related to genetics or traumatic experiences which change the way the brain works.
