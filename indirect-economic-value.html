7. give 10 example of species with their direct economic value,indirect economic value, aesthetic value​


1. Honeybees -

Direct economic value: honey, beeswax, pollination of cropsIndirect economic value: pollination of wild plants, contribution to ecosystem servicesAesthetic value: beauty of flowers and buzzing sound of bees.

2. Atlantic salmon -

Direct economic value: commercial fishing, recreational fishingIndirect economic value: contribution to ecosystem services, support for local economiesAesthetic value: beauty of the fish and their habitat.

3. Eucalyptus trees -

Direct economic value: timber, pulpwood, essential oilsIndirect economic value: carbon sequestration, soil conservationAesthetic value: beauty of the trees and their distinctive smell.

4. Tigers -

Direct economic value: ecotourism, sale of tiger parts on black marketIndirect economic value: contribution to ecosystem services, support for local economiesAesthetic value: beauty of the animal and its cultural significance.

5. Coffee plants -

Direct economic value: coffee beans, employment in the coffee industryIndirect economic value: soil conservation, carbon sequestrationAesthetic value: beauty of the plants and their fragrant flowers.

6. Coral reefs -

Direct economic value: tourism, commercial fishingIndirect economic value: contribution to ecosystem services, protection from stormsAesthetic value: beauty of the reefs and their diverse marine life.

7. Giant pandas -

Direct economic value: ecotourism, sale of panda-themed merchandiseIndirect economic value: contribution to ecosystem services, support for local economiesAesthetic value: beauty of the animal and its cultural significance.

8. Soybeans -

Direct economic value: food products, animal feed, biofuelsIndirect economic value: soil conservation, carbon sequestrationAesthetic value: beauty of the plants and their flowers.

9. Blue whales -

Direct economic value: ecotourism, commercial whaling (historically)Indirect economic value: contribution to ecosystem services, support for local economiesAesthetic value: beauty of the animal and its cultural significance.

10. Oak trees -

Direct economic value: timber, fuelwood, acorns for wildlifeIndirect economic value: carbon sequestration, soil conservationAesthetic value: beauty of the trees and their fall foliage.

15. If the species could produce products that is good source for consumption and it specifically impacts the economy, this value of species is categories as:A)Direct Economic ValueB)Indirect Economy Value C)Aesthetic Value D)Romantic Value ​


Answer:

A. Direct Economic Value

Direct economic value is assigned to products harvested or exploited. Consumptive use value is one type of direct economic value that is used to describe products that never make it to a money market such as firewood, wild game, fruits, and vegetables. Often the value of such goods is never factored into GDP and other economic indexes.
